# Rewritten Primary Question (Qur’an-Only Challenge)

<div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1470721015889908853" itemprop="description articleBody">
<p></p><h2><strong>Rewritten Primary Question (Qur’an-Only Challenge)</strong></h2><blockquote><p><strong>Where in the Qur’an does Allah explicitly declare that Apostle Paul was a false <em>Nabī</em> (prophet) or <em>Rasūl</em> (messenger), or that he corrupted the Injīl (Gospel) or the earlier Scriptures?</strong></p><p><strong>I am requesting evidence from the Qur’an alone—not Hadith, Tafsir, Sīrah, or later Islamic tradition.</strong></p><p><strong>If Muslims can produce a single explicit Qur’anic verse stating that Paul was a false messenger or that he altered the Biblical text, I am prepared to accept Islam.</strong></p><p><strong>If no such Qur’anic evidence exists, then the accusation that Paul corrupted Christianity is itself un-Qur’anic, theologically baseless, and contrary to Allah’s own testimony regarding the previous Scriptures.</strong></p></blockquote><hr/><h2><strong>Suggested Follow-Up Questions (Qur’an-Only, Scholarly and Logical)</strong></h2><h3><strong>1. On Paul Specifically</strong></h3><ol><li><p>Where does the Qur’an <strong>name Apostle Paul</strong> (<em>Bulus</em>) and condemn him by name?</p></li><li><p>If Paul was a false teacher, <strong>why does the Qur’an remain completely silent about him</strong>, despite naming Pharaoh, Haman, Abu Lahab, and others?</p></li><li><p>Why would Allah condemn lesser figures by name but <strong>fail to expose the alleged greatest corrupter of Christianity</strong>?</p></li></ol><hr/><h3><strong>2. On Corruption of the Bible</strong></h3><ol start="4"><li><p>Where does the Qur’an say <strong>the Injīl text was rewritten, edited, or altered</strong> by human hands?</p></li><li><p>Why does the Qur’an repeatedly refer to the <strong>Injīl as something already present and authoritative</strong> during Muhammad’s lifetime (Qur’an 5:46–47)?</p></li><li><p>If the Bible was corrupted <strong>before Islam</strong>, why does Allah command Jews and Christians to judge by it?</p></li></ol><hr/><h3><strong>3. On Allah’s Preservation of Revelation</strong></h3><ol start="7"><li><p>If Allah failed to preserve the Torah and Injīl, <strong>how can Muslims trust that Allah successfully preserved the Qur’an</strong>?</p></li><li><p>Does accusing Paul of corruption not imply <strong>Allah was either unable or unwilling to protect His own revelation</strong>?</p></li></ol><hr/><h3><strong>4. On Confirmation (Tasdiq)</strong></h3><ol start="9"><li><p>How can the Qur’an claim to <strong>“confirm” (yuṣaddiq)</strong> previous Scriptures (Qur’an 2:41; 3:3; 5:48) if those Scriptures were already corrupted?</p></li><li><p>Can a corrupted book logically serve as the <strong>standard of confirmation</strong> for a later revelation?</p></li></ol><hr/><h3><strong>5. On Historical Responsibility</strong></h3><ol start="11"><li><p>If Paul corrupted Christianity in the 1st century, <strong>why does the Qur’an—written six centuries later—never mention this catastrophic event?</strong></p></li><li><p>Why are Muslims relying on <strong>post-Qur’anic polemics</strong> to make accusations Allah Himself never made?</p></li></ol><hr/><h3><strong>6. On Theological Consistency</strong></h3><ol start="13"><li><p>Is it permissible in Islam to accuse a person of falsifying divine revelation <strong>without explicit Qur’anic evidence</strong>?</p></li><li><p>Would such accusations not fall under <strong>false testimony</strong>, which the Qur’an strictly condemns?</p></li></ol><hr/><h2><strong>Concluding Challenge Statement</strong></h2><blockquote><p><strong>Until Muslims can present explicit Qur’anic evidence that Apostle Paul was a false messenger or that he corrupted the Gospel, such claims remain theological innovations, not Qur’anic doctrine.</strong></p><p><strong>A faith that claims to restore truth must first remain faithful to its own Scripture.</strong></p></blockquote><hr/><p><br/></p>

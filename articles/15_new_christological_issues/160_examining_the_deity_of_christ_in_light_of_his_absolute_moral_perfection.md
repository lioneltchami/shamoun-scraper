# The Lord Jesus Christ – the Holy, Pure Son of God

**Examining the Deity of Christ in light of his absolute moral perfection**

[Sam Shamoun](https://www.youtube.com/@shamounian)

The website [www.muslimconverts.com](<http://www.muslimconverts.com/>) has published a transcript of a lecture that was given more than twenty five years ago by a Muslim apologist/polemicist named Steve Johnson (a.k.a. Farukh Abdallah). In the lecture, Mr. Johnson sought to discuss the evidence that Christians normally present to prove the Deity of the Lord Jesus Christ. His purpose was to show why the arguments of the Christians are inconclusive, being based on faulty logic and premises, and therefore do not prove that Jesus is God. 

One of the points which he tried to tackle is the issue of Jesus’ sinlessness or perfection and how Christians use this to prove that Christ is God. Johnson stated that:

Christians view Jesus as perfect. Their line of argumentation is very simple. Since only God is perfect and Jesus is perfect, then Jesus must be God.   
In logic we dismiss that kind of an argument, we say it is the fallacy of the undistributed middle term.   
  
Let me give you an example of that, to show you how illogical it is. If I said that Begin is tired and Reigan is tired then I would have to conclude that Begin is Reigan.  
  
So obviously it is an illogical kind of argument. It is the same kind of argument that we find there. You don´t even have to be a Major in logic to see that that is illogical.  
  
If you look to the Bible itself in the famous sermon on the mount in Matthew (chapter 5, verse 48), Jesus supposedly commands us to "be ye therefore perfect, as your Father who is in Heaven is perfect".  
  
Again Jesus of course was paraphrasing the Torah. We find those words in Leviticus (chapter 19, verse 2).  
  
But by commanding us to be perfect, or holy as it is said in Leviticus, to Jesus and God it means to indicate that we all have the potential to be God.  
  
Well of course no orthodox Christian would make such a claim. The fact is that if Jesus lived a perfect life, then he was a perfect man, but a perfect man is not God.  
  
Jesus was simply following the commandment revealed by God to Moses. (_Truth about Jesus_ , [source](<http://www.muslimconverts.com/converts/Farukh_Abdallah.htm> "Opens external link in new window"))

Anyone who is slightly familiar with the teaching of the Holy Bible can see that if anyone is committing a logical fallacy it is Johnson for distorting the Christian position and for essentially attacking a straw man. 

Moreover, apart from misrepresenting the Bible on the nature of Jesus’ perfection and holiness, the logical flaw of Johnson’s argument is evident in the second of the above quoted paragraphs. The crucial word is “only”. The tiredness of Begin and Reigan [_sic_] (Menachem Begin being the Prime Minister of Israel [1977-1983] and Ronald Reagan being the President of the USA [1981-1989] at the time of this lecture) does not establish their identity. If it could validly be said that _only_ Begin is tired, then Reagan being tired would establish that he is Begin. However, “only Begin is tired” is obviously wrong, so that Johnson’s attempted analogy breaks down before the discussion even starts. 

With that said, the purpose of this article is to show that, although God commands his followers to be perfect as he is, the fact is that no creature is capable of attaining such perfection since God alone is absolutely sinless and holy. This explains why every creature (angels included) needs the grace of God to save and preserve him/her. We will further show that, according to God’s inspired Writings, the Lord Jesus is absolutely perfect, being completely holy and righteous just as God. We will then follow that up with some closing remarks.

**Conclusion**

With the foregoing in perspective we can now clearly see that the Christian argument is logically sound. The Holy Bible, the inspired and inerrant Word of God, expressly and emphatically proclaims that God alone is absolutely pure, holy and righteous and that all of his creation is less than completely pure in his infinitely holy sight. At the same time, the inspired Scriptures affirm that Jesus is also absolutely pure, holy and righteous. Thus, Jesus must therefore be God! To put this in the form of a syllogism:

  1. Only Yahweh is completely pure, holy, righteous and sinless.
  2. Jesus is also completely pure, holy, righteous and sinless.
  3. Jesus is therefore Yahweh God (yet not the Father or the Holy Spirit).


Since the Holy Bible (as well as the Islamic corpus) affirms that both premises are true then the conclusion is also true and valid. After all, valid premises lead to valid conclusions which means that the Muslims cannot escape the logic of the inspired Scriptures that Jesus is God (the Son) since he is just as pure, holy, and righteous as God is (i.e. he is just as righteous and sinless as both God the Father and his eternal Holy Spirit are).

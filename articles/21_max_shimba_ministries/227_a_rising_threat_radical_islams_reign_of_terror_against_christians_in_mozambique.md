<h3><strong>A Rising Threat: Radical Islam’s Reign of Terror Against Christians in Mozambique</strong></h3>
<p>An extremely dangerous wave of persecution is sweeping across Mozambique as <strong>radical Islamist militants</strong> unleash a reign of terror on <strong>Christian believers</strong>. In the northern regions, entire villages have been burned, families displaced, and believers brutally murdered simply because of their faith in Jesus Christ.</p>
<p>Recent reports confirm that <strong>Christians are being driven from their homes, beaten, and even beheaded</strong>, their houses reduced to ashes. The chaos has forced tens of thousands to flee—many walking for days without food or water—seeking safety in makeshift refugee camps scattered across the country.</p>
<p>Among these suffering yet resilient believers are members of <strong>Afrika Wa Yesu</strong>, a Christian ministry deeply rooted in Mozambique’s rural mission fields. Missionaries <strong>Rodney and Ellie Hein</strong>, who have faithfully served for over 40 years, recently visited one of these refugee camps—home to more than <strong>60,000 displaced souls</strong>. There, they found a community not consumed by despair, but filled with the joy of Christ.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“They didn’t speak of their suffering; they spoke of joy over sharing the Gospel where they were,”<br/>
— <strong>Ellie Hein, Missionary to Mozambique</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p>This testimony stands as a powerful reminder that <strong>the light of the Gospel shines brightest in the darkest places</strong>. While radical Islam attempts to extinguish the faith of believers through fear and violence, these Mozambican Christians are <strong>turning tragedy into testimony</strong>—using their suffering as an opportunity to proclaim the saving power of Jesus Christ.</p>
<p>The situation remains dire. Entire regions are destabilized. Villages are ghost towns. Children are orphaned. Yet, amidst the ruins, <strong>the Church stands unbroken</strong>, holding fast to the hope that can never be silenced by terror.</p>
<p>At <strong>Shimba Theological Institute</strong>, we call upon all Christians worldwide to:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>Pray fervently</strong> for our brothers and sisters in Mozambique— for protection, provision, and unshakable faith.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Support missionaries</strong> and ministries providing relief and spiritual encouragement on the ground.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Raise awareness</strong> in churches and communities about the escalating persecution in Africa’s forgotten corners.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>The blood of the martyrs has always been the seed of the Church. And once again, from the soil of Mozambique, the Gospel is sprouting through tears, faith, and forgiveness.</p>
<p>As CBN News reports, these persecuted believers—some of whom have lost everything—are offering <strong>“powerful displays of forgiveness that point to Jesus.”</strong> Their lives testify that the love of Christ triumphs even over the sword of Islamism.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“Blessed are those who are persecuted because of righteousness, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.”<br/>
— <em>Matthew 5:10 (NIV)</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Let us stand united, in prayer and action, until the light of Christ prevails over every shadow of hate.</p>

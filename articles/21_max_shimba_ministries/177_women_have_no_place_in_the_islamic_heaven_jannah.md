<h2><strong>The Contradiction of Islamic Punishment for Adultery</strong></h2>
<p>The punishment for adultery in Islam has two conflicting laws:</p>
<h3><strong>1. Qur’anic Law</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Surah An-Nur 24:2</strong> commands:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>“Flog both the male and the female adulterer with 100 lashes, in the presence of the believers.”</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Qur’an gives <strong>the same punishment</strong> for:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>married adulterers</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>unmarried adulterers</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>But:</p>
<h3><strong>2. Hadith Law (Muhammad’s Law)</strong></h3>
<p>The Hadith—<em>Sahih al-Bukhari</em> Book 8, No. 805—teaches <strong>stoning to death</strong> for adulterers.</p>
<p>Most Qur’an translations avoid the word “stoning” because <strong>it is not found anywhere in the Arabic Qur’an</strong>, the original language. It only appears in Hadith.</p>
<p>So which is stronger?<br/>
<strong>Allah’s law in the Qur’an?<br/>
Or Muhammad’s law in the Hadith?</strong></p>
<p>Why did Muhammad’s law override Allah’s law?<br/>
Why did the 100-lashes punishment get replaced by stoning?</p>
<p>These contradictions expose a system that punishes women far more severely.</p>

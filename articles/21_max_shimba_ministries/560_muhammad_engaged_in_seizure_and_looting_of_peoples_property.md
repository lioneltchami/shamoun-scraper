<strong>MUHAMMAD ENGAGED IN SEIZURE AND LOOTING OF PEOPLE’S PROPERTY</strong></p><p><strong>A Behavior Contrary to a Prophet’s Conduct</strong></p><p>Historical records indicate that Muhammad, along with his followers, engaged in the seizure and looting of caravans belonging to merchants and travelers. This conduct raises significant questions about the prophetic character traditionally attributed to him.</p><p>According to <strong>Sahih Bukhari</strong>, Volume 3, Book 37, Chapter 8, Hadith 495 (page 280), it is stated: <em>“When Allah made the Prophet wealthy without conflict, one-fifth of the war captives’ possessions were allocated to the treasury.”</em> Similarly, <strong>Sahih Muslim</strong>, Volume 2, Book 5, Hadith 401, Number 2348 (page 519) indicates that Muhammad’s family retained shares of the spoils, illustrating that these acts contributed to their personal wealth.</p><p>The first organized seizures of people’s property by early Muslims are historically documented as the <strong>Nakha’ Raid</strong>. Even during the sacred months, when fighting was traditionally suspended, Muhammad’s followers attacked caravans, killing individuals and taking the survivors as captives. Muhammad personally led the second raid at <strong>Badr</strong>, consolidating both wealth and influence.</p><p>Muhammad further expanded his wealth through attacks on Jewish settlements, notably <strong>Khaybar</strong>. He and his loyal followers seized goods, women, and other property. Historical accounts record that after the surrender of 700–1,000 Jewish men from the <strong>Banu Quraiza</strong> tribe, they were executed, and their women were taken. This raises critical questions regarding the ethical and prophetic nature of such actions.</p><p>The evidence challenges the conventional narrative of Muhammad as solely a spiritual leader, showing that his rise was also facilitated through warfare, conquest, and appropriation of wealth.</p><p>—<br/>

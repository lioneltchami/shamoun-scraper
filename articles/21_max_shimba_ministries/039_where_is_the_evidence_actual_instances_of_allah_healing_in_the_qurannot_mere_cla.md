# Where is the evidence (actual instances) of Allah healing in the Qur’an—not mere claims or theological assertions?

**Source:** [Max Shimba Ministries](https://www.maxshimbaministries.org/2025/12/where-is-evidence-actual-instances-of.html)

**Category:** 21 Max Shimba Ministries

---

<div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4927185524274844677" itemprop="description articleBody">
<p></p><h2>1. The Core Problem: No Recorded Healing Event by Allah in the Qur’an</h2><p>In the <strong>Qur’an</strong>, there is <strong>no narrative account</strong> where:</p><ul><li><p>A named sick person is healed,</p></li><li><p>A disease is removed,</p></li><li><p>A blind person sees,</p></li><li><p>A dead person is raised,</p></li><li><p>A paralytic walks,</p></li><li><p>Or a physical illness is explicitly cured by Allah.</p></li></ul><p>There are <strong>claims about healing</strong>, but <strong>no documented healing events</strong>.</p><hr/><h2>2. Verses Commonly Cited — Examined Closely</h2><h3>A. Qur’an 26:80</h3><blockquote><p><em>“And when I am ill, it is He who cures me.”</em></p></blockquote><p><strong>Analysis:</strong></p><ul><li><p>This is <strong>Abraham’s statement</strong>, not a recorded miracle.</p></li><li><p>No illness is named.</p></li><li><p>No healing event occurs in the text.</p></li><li><p>This is a <strong>theological belief</strong>, not evidence.</p></li></ul><p>✔ Claim<br/>✘ No event<br/>✘ No witness<br/>✘ No result described</p><hr/><h3>B. Qur’an 10:57</h3><blockquote><p><em>“…a healing for what is in the breasts.”</em></p></blockquote><p><strong>Analysis:</strong></p><ul><li><p>“Healing” here refers to <strong>guidance, admonition, or inner reassurance</strong>.</p></li><li><p>Classical tafsir agrees this is <strong>spiritual/moral</strong>, not physical healing.</p></li><li><p>No sick person is healed.</p></li></ul><p>✔ Metaphorical<br/>✘ Physical healing<br/>✘ Event-based evidence</p><hr/><h3>C. Qur’an 16:69 (Honey verse)</h3><blockquote><p><em>“…there is healing for people.”</em></p></blockquote><p><strong>Analysis:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Honey is described as beneficial.</p></li><li><p><strong>Allah does not heal anyone directly in the text.</strong></p></li><li><p>This is closer to <strong>natural remedy</strong>, not divine intervention.</p></li><li><p>No miracle, no person, no outcome recorded.</p></li></ul><p>✔ Medicinal benefit<br/>✘ Divine healing act<br/>✘ Historical healing instance</p><hr/><h2>3. Jesus in the Qur’an — A Crucial Distinction</h2><p>The Qur’an <strong>does record healing acts</strong>, but <strong>not by Allah directly</strong>.</p><h3>Qur’an 3:49</h3><blockquote><p><em>Jesus heals the blind and the leper and raises the dead — “by Allah’s permission.”</em></p></blockquote><p><strong>Critical observation:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Healing <strong>does occur</strong>,</p></li><li><p>But <strong>Allah does not perform it directly</strong> in the narrative.</p></li><li><p>Jesus is the <strong>active healer</strong>, Allah is the <strong>authorizing authority</strong>.</p></li></ul><p>This still does <strong>not provide an example of Allah healing someone Himself</strong>.</p><hr/><h2>4. Contrast with the Bible (For Context, Not Polemics)</h2><p>In the Bible:</p><ul><li><p>Named individuals are healed (blind Bartimaeus, paralytics, lepers).</p></li><li><p>Diseases are specified.</p></li><li><p>Results are immediate and public.</p></li><li><p>Witnesses are present.</p></li><li><p>Events are narrated historically.</p></li></ul><p>In the Qur’an:</p><ul><li><p>No such narrative exists for Allah.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>5. Final Evidence-Based Conclusion</h2><p><strong>Based strictly on the Qur’anic text:</strong></p><ul><li><p>❌ No named sick person healed by Allah</p></li><li><p>❌ No disease removed by Allah in a narrative account</p></li><li><p>❌ No healing miracle performed directly by Allah</p></li><li><p>✔ Only <strong>claims</strong>, <strong>statements</strong>, or <strong>generalized descriptions</strong></p></li><li><p>✔ Healing acts appear <strong>only through Jesus</strong>, not Allah Himself</p></li></ul><h3>Therefore:</h3><blockquote><p><strong>The Qur’an contains claims about Allah as healer, but no recorded healing event that serves as empirical or narrative evidence.</strong></p></blockquote><p><br/></p>

# Between Jesus and Allah — Who Is Truly Al-Ba’ith (الباعث), "The Resurrection"?

**Source:** [Max Shimba Ministries](https://www.maxshimbaministries.org/2025/07/between-jesus-and-allah-who-is-truly-al_7.html)

**Category:** 21 Max Shimba Ministries

---

<div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-278959432938822030" itemprop="description articleBody">
<p></p>
<h3>One critical question every seeker of truth should ask is this: <strong>Between Jesus and Allah, who first identified Himself as <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>—"The Resurrection"?</strong></h3><p>
Whoever was the first to claim this title holds the rightful authority over it. Any subsequent use of the title would be a borrowed imitation, not a genuine claim to divine identity.</p>
<p>Over six centuries before Muhammad and the Quran, <strong>Jesus boldly declared Himself to be <em>Al-Ba’ith</em></strong>—The Resurrection:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>John 11:25</strong><br/>
<em>Jesus said to her, "I am the resurrection (<em>Al-Ba’ith</em> الباعث) and the life. Whoever believes in me, though he die, yet shall he live; 26 and everyone who lives and believes in me shall never die. Do you believe this?"</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In contrast, one of the 99 names of the Islamic Allah is <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>, which also means "The Resurrection." However, in Islamic theology, the name of Allah is inseparable from His essence—thus Allah <em>is</em> <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>.</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Quran, Surah Al-Hajj 22:5, 7</strong><br/>
<em>O mankind! If you are in doubt concerning the Resurrection, then verily We have created you from dust...</em><br/>
<em>And verily, the Hour will come, there is no doubt about it, and Allah will resurrect those who are in the graves.</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Yet, this verse in Surah Al-Hajj reveals a significant problem: the people of Muhammad’s time doubted Allah’s ability to resurrect because they had never witnessed Allah raising anyone from the dead!<br/>
<strong>How, then, can Allah claim the title <em>Al-Ba’ith</em> without demonstrating the power of resurrection in a tangible, witnessed event?</strong></p>
<p><strong>In contrast, Jesus provided irrefutable evidence of His power as <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>.</strong> He publicly raised Lazarus from the dead—an event recorded in Scripture and witnessed by many:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>John 11:23-25</strong><br/>
<em>Jesus said to her, "Your brother will rise again."</em><br/>
<em>Martha answered, "I know he will rise again in the resurrection at the last day."</em><br/>
<em>Jesus said to her, "I am the resurrection and the life."</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>And continuing in verse 43:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>John 11:43-44</strong><br/>
<em>When He had said these things, He cried out with a loud voice, "Lazarus, come out!" The man who had died came out, his hands and feet bound with linen strips, and his face wrapped with a cloth. Jesus said to them, "Unbind him, and let him go."</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In my own careful study of the Quran, I have found <strong>no account of Allah raising anyone from the dead—not even His beloved prophet Muhammad.</strong> The name <em>Al-Ba’ith</em> is merely proclaimed without any demonstrable miracle to support it.<br/>
<strong>Allah asks people to "believe" He will resurrect, but He never showed this power even once.</strong></p>
<p>Now we must confront the ultimate question:</p>
<p>👉 <strong>Between Jesus—who physically raised the dead—and Allah—who merely adopted the title without proof—who is the true <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>?</strong><br/>
👉 Is it <strong>Jesus</strong>, whose actions confirm His divine identity?<br/>
👉 Or <strong>Allah</strong>, whose title lacks demonstrated authority?</p>
<p>For me, the answer is clear: <strong>Jesus Christ is <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>—the Resurrection and the Life!</strong><br/>
He alone holds the power over life and death, proven by His works and His victory over the grave.</p>
<p><strong>Shalom.</strong><br/>
<em>Dr. Maxwell Shimba</em><br/>
Servant of Jesus Christ, the Great God. <em>(Titus 2:13)</em></p>

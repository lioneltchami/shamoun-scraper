To summarize, Jesus is said to be the Lord of all, the King of all, the God of all, and the One who protects his followers from the harmful affects of the evil one. Moreover, Christ is the All-holy, the One who is completely pure and righteous, the All-peaceable, the One who grants everlasting peace to his followers, the All-faithful, the One who never breaks his promises and can therefore be completely trusted. Jesus is also the All-preserver, the One who protects his flock forever, the All-mighty, the All-compeller, he whose love controls and guides his faithful to do all that he desires, and the All-supreme, the One having complete supremacy over all creation.

According to the Muslim scripture, all of these titles and functions belong exclusively to Allah, or God, and therefore proves that, according to the NT, Jesus is God (yet not the Father or the Holy Spirit).

Finally, we need to preface our use of the Quran lest the readers think that we somehow embrace its authority or reliability. We do not quote the Quran because we believe it is inspired or historically accurate, but we cite it to prove to Muslims who do believe in its inspiration and veracity that both Jesus and his followers claimed that Christ is God. The only way they can contest this fact is by undermining the textual veracity and historical reliability of the NT writings, something which would only pose more problems for the Muslim position ([*](/articles/11-answering_islam)).

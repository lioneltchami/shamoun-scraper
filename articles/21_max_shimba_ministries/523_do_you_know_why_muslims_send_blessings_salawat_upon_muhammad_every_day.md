<h3><strong>MUHAMMAD ASKED FOR FORGIVENESS ONE HUNDRED TIMES PER DAY</strong></h3>
<p>In <em>Sahih Muslim, Book 35, Number 6522</em>, it states that the Prophet Muhammad was so sinful that he asked Allah for forgiveness one hundred times every day. <em>Quote:</em></p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>“Al-Agharr al-Muzani, one of the companions of the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him), reported that the Messenger of Allah said: ‘Sometimes a veil is cast over my heart, and I seek forgiveness from Allah one hundred times a day.’”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Notice that the Islamic tradition not only declares Muhammad sinful, but also reveals that he felt a <em>veil</em>—a dark or troubling feeling—over his heart. Yet according to the Qur’an, Muhammad is supposed to be the perfect model for life and conduct.</p>
<p><strong>So how could Muhammad have a dark veil over his heart?</strong></p>
<p>Where did this heaviness come from, especially if he was receiving “revelation” from Allah?</p>
<p>Could it be possible that the so-called revelation itself was the darkness filling his heart?</p>

# Jesus Is the Christ — The Beginning of Christianity

**Category:** 21 Max Shimba Ministries

<h1><strong>By Dr. Maxwell Shimba | Shimba Theological Institute</strong></h1>
<p>Muslim friends often attempt to frame Jesus (Isa in Islam) as merely a prophet in a long line of prophets, subordinate to Muhammad, and a Muslim in practice. Yet such a claim collapses under the weight of historical truth and biblical theology. Let us examine carefully: <strong>Jesus is not a “Muslim” nor simply a “prophet” — Jesus is the CHRIST — the very foundation of Christianity, its beginning, and its living center.</strong></p>
<h3>1️⃣ Jesus is the <em>Christ</em> — Not Merely a Prophet</h3>
<p>The Greek word <em>Christos</em> (Χριστός) means <em>Anointed One,</em> equivalent to the Hebrew <em>Messiah</em> (משיח). In the Bible, Jesus is explicitly declared as the Christ:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“Simon Peter answered and said, <em>Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God</em>.”<br/>
— <strong>Matthew 16:16 (KJV)</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Qur’an never acknowledges Jesus as <em>the Christ</em> in the biblical sense — though it uses the title <em>al-Masih</em> without understanding its deep theological meaning. Islam strips the term of its Jewish and Christian messianic significance. Yet according to the Bible, the role of the Christ is utterly unique — He is:<br/>
✅ the fulfillment of prophecy (Isaiah 53, Daniel 9:24-27)<br/>
✅ the divine Son (Psalm 2:7, John 1:1-14)<br/>
✅ the Redeemer of mankind through the Cross (1 Corinthians 15:3-4)</p>
<h3>2️⃣ Jesus is the <em>Beginning of Christianity</em></h3>
<p>Without Jesus, there is no Christianity — for Christianity is not a system of law, not an ethnic culture, not merely moral teachings — it is the Gospel of the person and work of <strong>Jesus Christ</strong>.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“For other foundation can no man lay than that is laid, which is Jesus Christ.”<br/>
— <strong>1 Corinthians 3:11 (KJV)</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Christianity begins with Christ’s Incarnation, reaches its climax at the Cross and Resurrection, and proclaims His Kingdom until His return. No Jesus, no Cross, no Resurrection — no Christianity.</p>
<p>Islam, on the other hand, speaks of Isa as a forerunner to Muhammad — but this reverses the divine order. It is Jesus who is the apex of divine revelation:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“God… hath in these last days spoken unto us by His Son…”<br/>
— <strong>Hebrews 1:1-2 (KJV)</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<h3>3️⃣ Jesus is Not a <em>Christian</em> in the Sense of Being a Follower of Himself</h3>
<p>Another common Muslim confusion is to ask: “Was Jesus a Christian?”</p>
<p>This is a category error. A <em>Christian</em> is a <em>follower of Christ.</em> Jesus is not a follower of Himself — He <strong>is</strong> the Christ. He is the one we follow!</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by Me.”<br/>
— <strong>John 14:6 (KJV)</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p>He is the Head of the Body (Colossians 1:18), the firstborn from the dead (Revelation 1:5), and the Alpha and Omega (Revelation 22:13). <strong>Christians</strong> are named after Him because we belong to Him:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“And the disciples were called Christians first in Antioch.”<br/>
— <strong>Acts 11:26 (KJV)</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Thus, to suggest that Jesus is “Muslim” or a mere prophet in line with Muhammad utterly misses the reality: Jesus existed long before Muhammad, long before Islam, and is the fulfillment of <strong>God’s eternal redemptive plan</strong> (Ephesians 1:4-10).</p>
<h3>4️⃣ Conclusion to Muslim Readers</h3>
<p>Dear Muslim friends: recognize the unique identity of Jesus the Christ. Without Jesus, there is no Christianity. He is not “one of the prophets” awaiting Muhammad — He is the Lord, the Savior, the beginning and foundation of the faith that brings eternal life (John 3:16).</p>
<p>No Muhammad, no Islam — but without Jesus, there is no Christianity, no salvation, no Kingdom of God on earth.</p>
<p>It is time to stop reducing Jesus to a mere prophet. Receive Him for who He truly is:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>JESUS IS THE CHRIST — THE SON OF THE LIVING GOD — THE BEGINNING AND LORD OF CHRISTIANITY.</strong></p>
</blockquote>

<p>HAS ALLAH FABRICATED (DISTORTED) SCIENCE AND CREATION?</p>
<p><strong>1. The Qur’an allegedly anticipated modern science:</strong><br/>
It teaches that the sun and the moon each follow an orbit and revolve on their axis (Surah 21:33; 36:27–40). The Qur’an also supposedly teaches the Big Bang theory that caused the creation of the world (Surah 21:30; 51:47–48).<br/>
The Arabic word <em>falak</em> means “orbit.” Astronomers tell us that the sun revolves around the center of the galaxy once every 250 million years. The Arabic word <em>yasbahuna</em> (from <em>sabaha</em>) means “to swim,” but some Muslims claim it means “to rotate in its own course.”</p>
<p><strong>Response:</strong> This is daydreaming for two reasons:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>The words are not clear:</strong><br/>
<em>Falak</em> is an Arabic word that can mean path or flowing in a course, and <em>yasbahuna</em> can mean “to hurry,” even though its more straightforward meaning is “to swim.”</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Muhammad himself clearly told us what it means</strong>, and he meant that the sun and the moon travel <strong>above the earth</strong>, moving across a <strong>dome-like sky</strong> over the world.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>There are <strong>two primary Islamic sources</strong> that record Muhammad’s explanation:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“God created a sea three farsakhs (18 km) from heaven. The clouds are restrained, standing in the air by God’s command. None of its water falls. The seas do not move, but the sea moves with the speed of an arrow. It has the freedom to move in the air with equal measure, as if it were a stretched rope between east and west. The sun, the moon, and the returning stars [the five planets] run in the depths of its belly. This is what the word of God means: ‘Each one swims in its orbit.’ ‘The sky’ is the circle of chariots pulled by horses in the depths of that sea.”<br/>
— <em>Al-Tabari, vol. 1, p. 235.</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Abu Dharr narrated:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“One evening the Prophet asked me, ‘Do you know where the sun goes when it sets?’ I replied, ‘Allah and His Messenger know best.’ He said, ‘It goes (that is, it travels) until it prostrates beneath the Throne, and it seeks permission to rise again, and it is permitted. But a time will come when it will want to prostrate but its prostration will not be accepted…’”<br/>
— <em>Bukhari, vol. 4, book 54, chapter 4, no. 441, p. 283; Sahih Muslim, vol. 1, book 1, chapter 73, no. 297–300, pp. 95–96</em> (also gives a more detailed account).</p>
</blockquote>

<h2>CELEBRATING THE MAWLID (BIRTHDAY) OF PROPHET MUHAMMAD: PAGAN ROOTS AND ABSENCE IN THE QURAN</h2>
<p><strong>Max Shimba Ministries Org.</strong></p>
<hr/>
<h3>Introduction</h3>
<p>This article examines the historical, textual, and scholarly perspectives on the celebration of the Mawlid (Prophet Muhammad’s birthday). It interrogates the origin, lack of scriptural basis, and the critical stance of renowned Islamic scholars, arguing that the practice is an innovation (bid‘ah) rooted in pagan custom rather than Islamic revelation.</p>
<hr/>
<h3>1. The History of Mawlid</h3>
<p>Historical records indicate that neither the Prophet Muhammad nor his Companions celebrated the Mawlid. The earliest reports of the celebration emerge centuries after the Prophet's death, primarily among the Fatimid (Ismaili Shi‘a) dynasty in Egypt (Calvert, 2010; Schimmel, 1987).</p>
<p>Al-Hafidh al-Sakhawi (d. 902 AH) records:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The celebration of the Mawlid was not practiced by the pious predecessors (Salaf al-Salih) of the first three generations. It was introduced after them.”<br/>
(al-Sakhawi, <em>Subul al-Huda wa al-Rashad</em>, Vol. 1, p. 439)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Al-Maqrizi (d. 845 AH) provides a historical account:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The Fatimid rulers inaugurated the Mawlid festival along with others, such as Mawlid of ‘Ali, Fatima, Hasan, Husayn, and their living Imam, as well as other seasonal festivals.”<br/>
(al-Maqrizi, <em>al-Khitat</em>, Vol. 1, p. 490)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Further, Shaykh ‘Ali Mahfudh notes:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The first to introduce Mawlid in Cairo were the Fatimid rulers in the 4th century AH. They created six Mawlids.”<br/>
(Mahfudh, <em>al-Ibda‘ fi Madhahir al-Ibtida‘</em>, p. 251)</p>
</blockquote>
<h4>Sunni Adoption</h4>
<p>Sheikh Abdulla Saleh Farsy states:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The first official Mawlid recited by Sunnis was organized by King Mudhaffar ad-Din in northern Iraq, a brother-in-law to Sultan Salahuddin al-Ayyubi. This event was unprecedented in size and splendor.”<br/>
(Farsy, <em>Tafsiri ya Mawlid Barzanji</em>, p. iv)</p>
</blockquote>
<hr/>
<h3>2. Absence of Scriptural Evidence</h3>
<p>There is no authentic evidence from the Quran or the sahih hadith specifying the exact date of the Prophet's birth or instructing Muslims to commemorate it.</p>
<p>Safi-ur-Rahman Mubarakpuri writes:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“Muhammad… was born on Monday, 9th Rabi‘ al-Awwal, in the Year of the Elephant… corresponding to April 20 or 22, 571 CE.”<br/>
(Mubarakpuri, <em>Ar-Raheeq al-Makhtum</em>, p. 62)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Sirajur Rahman adds:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The event of the Elephant occurred in Muharram, fifty-five days before the Prophet’s birth, suggesting the birth fell between 25th Safar and 25th Rabi‘ al-Awwal.”<br/>
(Rahman, <em>Al-Mustafa</em>, p. 11)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Quran remains silent on the Prophet’s exact birth date and does not prescribe any celebration of his birth (Nasr, 2003).</p>
<hr/>
<h3>3. Scholarly Critique and the Question of Innovation (Bid‘ah)</h3>
<p>Notable classical and contemporary scholars have denounced the Mawlid as an innovation with pagan undertones:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Imam al-Shatibi (d. 790 AH)</strong>: Criticized the Mawlid as an innovation in his magnum opus.<br/>
(al-Shatibi, <em>al-I‘tisam</em>, Vol. 1, p. 34)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Imam al-Fakihani (d. 734 AH)</strong>: Authored a treatise refuting the legitimacy of Mawlid.<br/>
(al-Fakihani, <em>Risalah fi Mawlid al-Nabi</em>)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Imam Ibn al-Hajj al-Maliki (d. 737 AH)</strong>: Explicitly categorized Mawlid as bid‘ah.<br/>
(Ibn al-Hajj, <em>al-Madkhal</em>, Vol. 2, pp. 11–12)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Abu ‘Abdillah al-Haffar al-Maliki</strong>: Asserted the absence of Mawlid celebration among the Prophet’s Companions or their successors.<br/>
(al-Haffar, <em>al-Mi‘yar al-Mu‘rab</em>, Vol. 7, p. 99)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Abu At-Tayyib Shams al-Haq al-‘Aẓimabadi</strong> and his teacher Bashiruddin Qannuji authored a monograph refuting the practice.<br/>
(Qannuji, <em>Ghayat al-Kalam fi Ibtal Amal al-Mawlid wal-Qiyam</em>)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The Prophet himself is reported to have said:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“Whoever introduces into our religion that which is not from it, it will be rejected.”<br/>
(Sahih al-Bukhari, Hadith 2697; Sahih Muslim, Hadith 1718)</p>
</blockquote>

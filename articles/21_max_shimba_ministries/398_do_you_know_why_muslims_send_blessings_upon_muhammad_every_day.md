<p>In his book "End of the Front Line," Reza Safa F writes, citing a hadith where Muhammad’s wife Khadija asked him to seek forgiveness for his sins, Muhammad replied that he himself was not sure his own sins had been forgiven! How could he forgive someone else’s sins?</p>
<p><strong>MUHAMMAD ASKED FOR FORGIVENESS ONE HUNDRED TIMES A DAY:</strong></p>
<p>In “Sahih Muslim, Book 35, Hadith 6522,” it says that the Prophet Muhammad was so sinful that he would ask Allah for forgiveness a hundred times every day.<br/>
<em>Quote:</em><br/>
“Today is today, whoever says tomorrow is a liar.”<br/>
My brothers and sisters, the more I study Islam, the more doubts I have, because there is nothing good at all in this religion of Muhammad and Jibril (Gabriel). Tell me, if a person asks for forgiveness one hundred times a day, what kind of sins do you think he has committed to be so afraid like this?</p>
<p>“Al-Agharr al-Muzani, who was one of the companions of the (Prophet), reported that the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) said: ‘Sometimes there is a veil upon my heart, and I seek forgiveness from Allah one hundred times a day.’”</p>
<p>Notice that the tradition not only says that Muhammad was very sinful, but that he felt a shadow, a bad feeling, over his heart. In Muhammad’s case, this is difficult because the Qur’an says that he is an example in his life and actions:</p>
<p>So, how could Muhammad have a shadow over his heart?<br/>
How did he get this distress while he was supposedly receiving revelation from Allah?<br/>
Is it possible that this revelation was itself the source of the distress filling his heart?</p>
<p><strong>Qur’an 68:4:</strong><br/>
"Your life and your deeds are [an example]." (Mohammed Knut Bernstöm, Qur’an)</p>
<p><strong>All people have sinned</strong><br/>
The Bible says that all people have sinned. This includes the Prophet Muhammad—he was sinful like everyone else, but Muhammad himself admitted that his sins were not forgiven even though he asked for forgiveness a hundred times a day.</p>
<p>Dear reader, why should we follow or listen to Muhammad, who admitted that his sins were not forgiven?<br/>
Why did Allah fail to forgive Muhammad’s sins?<br/>
Does Allah have the power to forgive sins?<br/>
Muhammad is the only prophet who died with sin, and the wages of sin is death in Hell. Romans 6:23:<br/>
“For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord.”<br/>
Because the sinner is condemned to death. “For the wages of sin is death” (Romans 6:23).<br/>
This means separation from God in Hell forever. “Just as people are destined to die once, and after that to face judgment” (Hebrews 9:27).</p>
<p>Therefore, why die in your sins like Muhammad and be separated from God in Hell?</p>

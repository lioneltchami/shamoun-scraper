<p></p><h1><strong>Why Did Aisha Give Her Flower to Safwan Ibn Al-Muattal?</strong></h1><p><em>By Dr. Maxwell Shimba, Shimba Theological Institute</em></p><p>This subject remains one of the most troubling issues within the Muslim community. When the “Mother of the Believers,” Aisha, engages in an act that raises shame and countless unanswered questions, one must ask: <strong>Did Aisha truly love Muhammad?</strong> Or had she already been accustomed to questionable conduct from the beginning? Personally, many concerning questions arise in my mind—questions that demand careful investigation. Did Aisha grow tired of Muhammad and decide to give her “flower” to Safwan Ibn Al-Muattal? And why did she refuse to bear Muhammad a child? These are legitimate queries.</p><p>Ibn Hisham, citing Ibn Ishaq, narrates that Aisha was caught committing <strong>ZINAA (sexual intercourse)</strong> with Safwan Ibn Al-Muattal, one of Muhammad’s companions—what is widely known as the <em>Safwan Incident</em>. This occurred in Madinah, and the news spread throughout the entire city, forcing the Prophet of Allah to return Aisha to her parents’ home in humiliation.</p><p>Ali, who was both Muhammad's cousin and the husband of his daughter Fatimah, advised Muhammad to <strong>divorce Aisha</strong> to hide the great embarrassment caused by being caught in an act of adultery.</p><p>The Muslims of Madinah, led by Abdullah bin Ubayy, publicly declared that Lady Aisha (RA) had committed adultery with Safwan. When Muhammad heard this, he gathered his companions and consulted them on what should be done. Some advised him to divorce her. The Prophet then visited Aisha and told her:<br/>
<strong>“If you are innocent, Allah will forgive you; but if not, seek forgiveness from your Lord.”</strong></p><p>Aisha cried uncontrollably and asked her parents for forgiveness for the <strong>immorality</strong> she had committed with Safwan, but her parents remained speechless.</p><h3><strong>References</strong></h3><ol>

<h3>JESUS AS THE SON OF GOD: WHAT DOES IT MEAN?</h3>
<p><strong>By Dr. Maxwell Shimba – Shimba Theological Institute</strong><br/>
<em>(Originally written August 15, 2015)</em></p>
<h4>Introduction</h4>
<p>One of the most persistent questions raised by Muslim scholars and believers concerns the Christian confession that <strong>Jesus Christ is the Son of God</strong>. Muslims often ask:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p><em>How can Jesus be the Son of God?</em></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>How can God have a Son without a wife?</em></p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>These questions reveal a misunderstanding of the Christian doctrine of divine sonship. The phrase <em>“Son of God”</em> does not imply a biological relationship or a physical act of procreation, as understood in human terms. Instead, it signifies a profound theological and spiritual truth rooted in divine revelation.</p>
<hr/>
<h3>1. How Is Jesus the Son of God?</h3>
<p>Jesus Christ is not the Son of God through human parentage or natural generation. God did not marry or engage in a physical relationship with Mary to produce a son. The Bible clearly rejects any notion of divine-human sexual union. Rather, Jesus’ sonship expresses <strong>God’s self-revelation in human form</strong>.</p>
<p>As the Gospel of John affirms:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God… The Word became flesh and dwelt among us” (John 1:1, 14).</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Thus, the title “Son of God” emphasizes that Jesus is <strong>God manifested in the flesh</strong>—the visible image of the invisible God.</p>
<hr/>
<h3>2. How Can God Have a Son Without a Wife?</h3>
<p>This question presupposes a human and material understanding of divine sonship. According to <strong>Luke 1:35</strong>, the angel Gabriel explained the mystery of Christ’s conception to Mary:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you; therefore the child to be born will be called holy, the Son of God.”</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Here, the Scripture explicitly states that <strong>Jesus’ conception was by the Holy Spirit</strong>, not through human means. The divine power of the Holy Spirit, who is God, brought about the incarnation of the Son. Therefore, Jesus is called <em>the Son of God</em> because His origin is divine.</p>
<p>Mary was told that the holy child she would bear would be called <strong>“the Son of God”</strong>—not because of a biological act, but because of the supernatural act of divine creation within her womb.</p>
<hr/>
<h3>3. Jesus Affirms His Sonship Before the High Priest</h3>
<p>During His trial before the Jewish High Priest, Jesus was directly questioned about His divine identity:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The high priest said to Him, ‘I charge You under oath by the living God: Tell us if You are the Christ, the Son of God.’ Jesus replied, ‘You have said it yourself; but I tell you, from now on you will see the Son of Man seated at the right hand of Power and coming on the clouds of heaven’” (Matthew 26:63–64).</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In this passage, <strong>Jesus did not deny being the Son of God</strong>. His response, instead, confirmed it. The Jewish leaders understood this as <strong>a claim to divinity</strong> and accused Him of blasphemy (Matthew 26:65–66).</p>
<hr/>
<h3>4. The Jewish Acknowledgment of Jesus’ Claim</h3>
<p>When Jesus was brought before Pontius Pilate, the Jewish leaders declared:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“We have a law, and according to that law He must die because He claimed to be the Son of God” (John 19:7).</p>
</blockquote>
<p>This verse is significant because it demonstrates that <strong>even the Jewish accusers recognized that Jesus claimed divine sonship</strong>. Their accusation of blasphemy arose precisely because they understood <em>“Son of God”</em> to mean <em>“equal with God.”</em></p>
<p>In Jewish thought, to call oneself the Son of God was to claim <strong>oneness in nature and essence with God Himself</strong>—a claim intolerable to those who rejected His divinity.</p>
<p>The writer of Hebrews affirms this same truth:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The Son is the radiance of God’s glory and the exact representation of His being, sustaining all things by His powerful word” (Hebrews 1:3).</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Thus, Jesus’ title as <em>the Son of God</em> expresses His <strong>divine essence, eternal relationship with the Father, and His participation in divine glory</strong>.</p>
<hr/>
<h3>5. The Meaning of “Son” as Manifestation, Not Biology</h3>
<p>In Scripture, the term <em>“son”</em> is sometimes used metaphorically to describe character or nature rather than literal descent. For example, in <strong>John 17:12</strong>, Judas Iscariot is called <em>“the son of perdition,”</em> not because perdition gave birth to him, but because <strong>he embodied destruction</strong>.</p>
<p>Similarly, Jesus as the <em>Son of God</em> means He <strong>manifests God’s nature and essence</strong>. He is God revealing Himself in human form—divine truth made visible to humankind.</p>
<hr/>
<h3>Conclusion</h3>
<p>This theological exploration clarifies that the Christian confession—<em>“Jesus is the Son of God”</em>—does not imply physical generation but expresses <strong>divine revelation and incarnation</strong>. Jesus is God manifest in the flesh, conceived by the Holy Spirit, and acknowledged even by His accusers as claiming equality with God.</p>
<p>Therefore, the question that Muslims frequently raise—<em>“How can God have a Son?”</em>—finds its answer in Scripture:<br/>
God’s Sonship is <strong>not biological but spiritual and ontological</strong>. It reveals the mystery of God’s love, self-expression, and redemption through Jesus Christ.</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Truly, Jesus is the Son of God.</strong></p>
</blockquote>

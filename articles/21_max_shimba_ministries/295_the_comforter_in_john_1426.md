<h3><strong>The Comforter in John 14:26: A Biblical and Islamic Analysis</strong></h3>
<p><strong>By Dr. Maxwell Shimba, Shimba Theological Institute</strong></p>
<h4><strong>1. The Identity of the Comforter (Paraklētos) in John 14:26</strong></h4>
<p>John 14:26 (KJV) reads:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>“But the Comforter, which is the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance, whatsoever I have said unto you.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>This passage is part of Jesus’ farewell discourse in the Gospel of John. The Greek term <em>Paraklētos</em> (παράκλητος) is translated as “Comforter,” “Advocate,” or “Helper.” The verse explicitly identifies this figure as <strong>“the Holy Ghost” (Holy Spirit)</strong> who will:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Be sent by <strong>the Father</strong></p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Come <strong>in Jesus’ name</strong></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Teach</strong> all things</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Remind</strong> the disciples of what Jesus said</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The Holy Spirit, therefore, is a divine person within the Trinitarian framework of Christian theology. The Holy Spirit's role is <strong>the continuation of Christ's presence</strong> and teaching among the disciples, not a new prophetic mission that contradicts or supersedes the work of Christ.</p>
<hr/>
<h4><strong>2. Was Muhammad Sent in the Name of Jesus?</strong></h4>
<p>To assess whether Prophet Muhammad was sent “in Jesus’ name,” one must evaluate both Islamic and biblical evidence.</p>
<p><strong>John 14:26</strong> says the Comforter comes <em>"in my [Jesus'] name."</em> This implies representation, continuation, and alignment with Jesus’ teachings and authority.</p>
<p>However, <strong>nowhere in the Quran</strong> does Allah state that:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Muhammad was sent <em>in the name of Jesus.</em></p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Muhammad would remind people of the sayings of Jesus.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Muhammad's mission was to represent or continue Jesus' ministry.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Rather, the Quran declares that Muhammad was sent <strong>as a warner to all people</strong> (Quran 7:158) and emphasizes his <strong>distinct prophethood</strong>, not one under the authority or name of Jesus.</p>
<hr/>
<h4><strong>3. Is Allah the Father of Jesus?</strong></h4>
<p>The Quran emphatically denies the <strong>fatherhood of Allah</strong> in any sense akin to the biblical understanding:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>“It is not befitting to (the majesty of) Allah that He should beget a son.”</em><br/>
(Quran 19:35)</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>“Say: He is Allah, the One... He begets not, nor was He begotten.”</em><br/>
(Quran 112:1–3)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In contrast, Christian theology, based on scriptures like Matthew 3:17 and John 3:16, affirms God as the <strong>Father of Jesus Christ</strong>, both ontologically and relationally. Thus, the divine Father who sends the Comforter in John 14:26 <strong>cannot be equated with Allah</strong> of the Quran, who denies having a son.</p>
<hr/>
<h4><strong>4. Does the Quran Mention a Comforter Sent by Allah?</strong></h4>
<p>The <strong>Quran does not mention</strong> a “Comforter” (<em>Paraklētos</em>) or any similar figure described in the Gospel of John.</p>
<p>Muslim apologists have historically attempted to claim that <strong>Muhammad is the “Comforter” (Paraklētos)</strong> referred to in John 14–16. This is based on a speculative linguistic argument that <em>Paraklētos</em> was originally <em>Periklutos</em> ("praised one") and thus refers to <strong>Ahmad</strong>, another name of Muhammad mentioned in Quran 61:6:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>“Jesus, the son of Mary, said: O Children of Israel! I am the messenger of Allah to you, confirming that which was [revealed] before me in the Torah and bringing good news of a messenger who will come after me, whose name will be Ahmad.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>However, no <strong>manuscript evidence</strong> from the Greek New Testament supports this textual corruption. All known Greek manuscripts read <em>Paraklētos</em>, not <em>Periklutos</em>.</p>
<p>Furthermore, the Comforter in John 14:26 is:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>The <strong>Holy Spirit</strong>, not a man</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Sent by <strong>the Father</strong>, not by Jesus himself</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Indwelling</strong> believers forever (John 14:16)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>These attributes <strong>do not fit Muhammad</strong>, who was a human prophet who did not indwell believers nor came in Jesus’ name.</p>
<hr/>
<h4><strong>5. Does the Quran Say Muhammad is a Comforter?</strong></h4>
<p>No. The <strong>Quran never refers to Muhammad</strong> using the Greek concept of <em>Paraklētos</em> (Comforter, Advocate, or Helper).</p>
<p>The Quran uses terms like:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>“Nadhir”</strong> (Warner) – Quran 33:45</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>“Bashir”</strong> (Bearer of good news) – Quran 33:45</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>“Abd”</strong> (Servant) – Quran 18:1</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>But never does it equate Muhammad with the <strong>Holy Spirit</strong> or describe his mission in the <strong>Johannine terms</strong> used for the Comforter.</p>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>Conclusion</strong></h3>
<p>There is <strong>no biblical or Quranic basis</strong> to identify Muhammad as the Comforter of John 14:26. The Comforter is clearly identified as the <strong>Holy Spirit</strong>, who proceeds from the <strong>Father in the name of Jesus</strong>, to continue Christ’s ministry by indwelling believers.</p>
<p>Furthermore:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Allah is not the Father</strong> of Jesus in Islam.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Muhammad was not sent in Jesus’ name.</strong></p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The <strong>Quran does not mention</strong> any figure equivalent to the <em>Paraklētos</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The <strong>Quran does not call Muhammad a “Comforter.”</strong></p>
</li>
</ul>

<h1><em>By Dr. Maxwell Shimba | Shimba Theological Institute</em></h1>
<hr/>
<h2><strong>An Important Question: Who Is the True <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>?</strong></h2>
<p>One critical question that every seeker of truth must ask is this:<br/>
👉 <strong>Between Jesus and Allah, who first identified Himself as <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>—"The Resurrection"?</strong></p>
<p>Whoever was the first to claim this divine title holds rightful authority over it.<br/>
Any later usage of the title would simply be an imitation, not an authentic claim.</p>
<hr/>
<h2><strong>Jesus: The First to Declare Himself <em>Al-Ba’ith</em></strong></h2>
<p>Over <strong>six centuries before Muhammad and the Quran</strong>, <strong>Jesus boldly declared</strong>:<br/>
<strong>“I am the Resurrection” (<em>Al-Ba’ith</em>).</strong></p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>John 11:25-26</strong><br/>
<em>Jesus said to her, "I am the resurrection (<em>Al-Ba’ith</em> الباعث) and the life. Whoever believes in me, though he die, yet shall he live; and everyone who lives and believes in me shall never die. Do you believe this?"</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Here, Jesus makes a direct and clear claim—not metaphorical, not future tense—<strong>He is the Resurrection Himself.</strong></p>
<hr/>
<h2><strong>The Quran’s Claim: Allah as <em>Al-Ba’ith</em></strong></h2>
<p>In Islam, one of <strong>Allah’s 99 names</strong> is <strong>Al-Ba’ith</strong>, meaning <strong>“The Resurrector” or “The Resurrection.”</strong><br/>
Since Allah’s names are inseparable from His essence, <strong>Allah = Al-Ba’ith.</strong></p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Quran, Surah Al-Hajj 22:5, 7</strong><br/>
<em>O mankind! If you are in doubt concerning the Resurrection, then verily We have created you from dust...</em><br/>
<em>And verily, the Hour will come, there is no doubt about it, and Allah will resurrect those who are in the graves.</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>However, there’s a theological problem here:<br/>
👉 <strong>The people of Muhammad’s time doubted Allah’s ability to resurrect, precisely because they had never seen Him raise anyone from the dead!</strong><br/>
👉 <strong>There was no evidence, no recorded miracle.</strong></p>
<p>How, then, can Allah rightfully claim the title <em>Al-Ba’ith</em> without ever performing a resurrection?</p>
<hr/>
<h2><strong>The Evidence of Jesus: A Public Resurrection</strong></h2>
<p>By contrast, <strong>Jesus provided public, irrefutable evidence</strong> that He is <em>Al-Ba’ith</em> by raising <strong>Lazarus</strong> from the dead—<strong>witnessed</strong> by many:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>John 11:23-25</strong><br/>
<em>Jesus said to her, "Your brother will rise again."</em><br/>
<em>Martha said, "I know that he will rise again in the resurrection at the last day."</em><br/>
<em>Jesus said to her, "I am the resurrection and the life."</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>And in <strong>John 11:43-44</strong>, the miracle unfolds:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>When He had said these things, He cried out with a loud voice, "Lazarus, come out!" The man who had died came out, his hands and feet bound with linen strips, and his face wrapped with a cloth. Jesus said to them, "Unbind him, and let him go."</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>This event occurred <strong>632 years before Muhammad</strong> and <strong>before the Quran was revealed</strong>.<br/>
👉 Jesus did not merely claim the title—<strong>He proved it</strong> through miraculous action.</p>
<hr/>
<h2><strong>What Does the Quran Show?</strong></h2>
<p>In my careful study of the Quran, I have found:<br/>
👉 <strong>No account of Allah raising anyone from the dead—not even Muhammad himself.</strong><br/>
👉 The title <em>Al-Ba’ith</em> in the Quran remains <strong>unsupported by evidence</strong>.<br/>
👉 Allah asks people to “believe” He will resurrect the dead—but no demonstration was given, either during Muhammad’s lifetime or in the Quranic narrative.</p>
<hr/>
<h2><strong>The Ultimate Question</strong></h2>
<p>So now, dear reader, consider:</p>
<p>👉 <strong>Between Jesus—who physically raised the dead—and Allah—who merely adopted the title without proof—who is the true <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>?</strong></p>
<p>✅ Is it <strong>Jesus</strong>, whose actions confirm His divine authority?<br/>
❌ Or <strong>Allah</strong>, whose claim lacks demonstrated power?</p>
<hr/>
<h2><strong>The Verdict</strong></h2>
<p>For me, and for all who seek truth, the answer is crystal clear:</p>
<p><strong>Jesus Christ is <em>Al-Ba’ith</em>—the Resurrection and the Life!</strong><br/>
He alone holds the power over life and death, proven by His works, His own resurrection, and His eternal victory over the grave.</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Titus 2:13</strong><br/>
<em>"...looking for the blessed hope and glorious appearing of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ."</em></p>
</blockquote>

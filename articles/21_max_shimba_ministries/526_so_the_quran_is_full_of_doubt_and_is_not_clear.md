<h2>SO, THE QURAN IS FULL OF DOUBT AND IS NOT CLEAR</h2>
<p><em>Image may contain: meme and text</em></p>
<p>Are the claims that “the Quran is complete and clear” true?</p>
<p>Allah reveals verses claiming that the Quran is <strong>not complete</strong> and <strong>not clear</strong>.</p>
<p>Allah says that there are verses in the Quran whose meaning <strong>no one knows except Allah</strong>.</p>
<p>Dear readers:</p>
<p>Today we will examine the confusion found inside the Quran—despite many Muslim scholars claiming that it is straightforward—while Allah Himself contradicts them by revealing verses that state the Quran is <strong>not straightforward</strong> and <strong>not clear</strong>.</p>
<p>First, let us look at the verses claiming that the Quran is clear, fully explained, and a guidance for the rightly guided:</p>
<p><strong>Surah Hud (11:1)</strong><br/>
<em>Alif Lam Ra. This is a Book whose verses have been made clear, then detailed, from One who is Wise and All-Aware.</em></p>
<p><strong>Surah Al-An’am (6:114)</strong><br/>
<em>"Shall I seek a judge other than Allah, while He is the One who has sent down to you the Book explained in detail? Those whom We gave the Scripture know it has been sent down from your Lord in truth. So do not be among the doubters."</em></p>
<p><strong>Surah An-Nahl (16:89)</strong><br/>
<em>"And [warn of] the Day when We will raise from every nation a witness against them, and We will bring you as a witness over these people. And We have sent down to you the Book explaining everything, as guidance, mercy, and glad tidings for the Muslims."</em></p>
<p>The above Surahs claim that the Quran is <strong>fully explained</strong>, <strong>clear</strong>, and that when you read it, you should not encounter confusion or contradictions.</p>
<hr/>
<h2>BUT THESE CLAIMS CONTRADICT THE CONFUSION FOUND IN ANOTHER VERSE, WHICH CLAIMS THAT <strong>ONLY ALLAH KNOWS THE MEANING OF SOME VERSES</strong></h2>
<p>Let us read this verse:</p>
<p><strong>Surah Al-Imran (3:7)</strong><br/>
<em>"He is the One who has sent down to you the Book. Within it are verses that are clear and decisive—these are the foundation of the Book. But others are ambiguous. As for those in whose hearts is deviation, they follow what is ambiguous, seeking discord and seeking its interpretation. But <strong>no one knows its interpretation except Allah</strong>. And those firmly grounded in knowledge say, ‘We believe in it; all is from our Lord.’ But none take heed except those with understanding."</em></p>
<p>Dear brothers and sisters, this is another disaster in Islam. In Surah Al-Imran—revealed in Medina—Allah says:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>In the Quran there are <em>muhkamat</em>—clear verses with obvious meaning.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>There are ambiguous verses, and those who follow them do so seeking discord.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>There are verses whose meaning <strong>no one knows except Allah</strong>.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>This Surah 3:7 contradicts Surah Hud 11:1, Surah Al-An’am 6:114, and Surah An-Nahl 16:89—which claim the Quran is <strong>clear</strong> and <strong>fully explained</strong>.</p>
<hr/>

<p>What do Muslims believe about a Quran that <strong>contradicts itself</strong> and <strong>is not clear</strong>?</p>

<p>How do such verses help Muslims?</p>
<p>What benefit do Muslims gain from reading verses whose meaning only Allah knows?</p>

<h2>Finally, I want to know:</h2>
<p>How do Muslims know the verses they read are meant to be understood—when Allah has set a trap by saying that some verses in the Quran are known <strong>only</strong> to Him?</p>

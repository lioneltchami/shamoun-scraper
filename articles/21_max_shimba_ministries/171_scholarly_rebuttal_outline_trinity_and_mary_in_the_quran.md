<h1>Scholarly Rebuttal Outline: Trinity and Mary in the Qur’an</h1>
<h3><strong>1. Qur’anic Claim</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Surah al-Ma’idah 5:116 suggests Christians worship a trinity of <strong>Allah, Jesus, and Mary</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>This forms the basis of the Qur’an’s alleged critique of Christianity.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>2. Historical Christian Doctrine</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Trinity</strong>: One God in three persons — Father, Son, Holy Spirit (Matthew 28:19; 2 Corinthians 13:14).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Mary</strong>: Blessed among women, honored as <em>Theotokos</em> (Mother of God in Christ’s humanity), never divine.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Evidence</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Nicene Creed (325 A.D.) – Father, Son, Holy Spirit. Mary absent.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Athanasian Creed – Confirms Trinitarian orthodoxy.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Church Fathers (Irenaeus, Tertullian, Athanasius) – No doctrine of Mary as God.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Conclusion</strong>: Qur’anic claim is historically and theologically false.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>3. Logical/Theological Analysis</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p>God is truth (Titus 1:2; Numbers 23:19).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>If the Qur’an were revelation from God, it could not misrepresent Christian belief.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Qur’an’s misrepresentation = <strong>logical impossibility for a true omniscient God</strong>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>4. Implications for Islam</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Allah in the Qur’an:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Misrepresents historical and theological facts.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Demonstrates either ignorance or deception.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Muhammad as prophet:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Claims false revelation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Therefore cannot be a messenger of the true God (Deuteronomy 18:20–22).</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>5. Scriptural Affirmation of Truth</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Bible confirms:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>God cannot lie (Titus 1:2).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>God revealed Himself as Father, Son, Holy Spirit.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Jesus Christ is the eternal Word and Savior (John 1:1–14).</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>6. Debate Strategy</strong></h3>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>Ask the Muslim scholar</strong>: “Have any Christians ever taught that Mary is God?”</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Cite historical evidence</strong>: Church councils, creeds, writings of early Church Fathers.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Expose the logical flaw</strong>: Omniscient God would not misrepresent human belief.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Contrast with Scripture</strong>: Highlight God’s truthfulness and Christ’s divine revelation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Conclude firmly</strong>: Qur’an misrepresents, Allah cannot be God, Muhammad cannot be true prophet, only Jesus is Savior and God.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>7. Optional Closing Question</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p>“If God cannot lie, how can the Qur’an claim Christians worship Mary, when history and theology clearly prove they do not?”</p>
</li>
</ul>

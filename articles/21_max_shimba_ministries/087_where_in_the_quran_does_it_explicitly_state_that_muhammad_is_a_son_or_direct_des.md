# Where in the Qur’an does it explicitly state that Muhammad is a son or direct descendant of Abraham, or where Abraham himself declares Muhammad as his descendant?

**Source:** [Max Shimba Ministries](https://www.maxshimbaministries.org/2025/12/where-in-quran-does-it-explicitly-state.html)

**Category:** 21 Max Shimba Ministries

---

<div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2671212024575267358" itemprop="description articleBody">
<p> <strong>Professional, Text-Based Analysis</strong></p><p><strong>Question:</strong> <em>Where in the Qur’an does it explicitly state that Muhammad is a son or direct descendant of Abraham, or where Abraham himself declares Muhammad as his descendant?</em></p><p><strong>Answer:</strong><br/>There is <strong>no verse in the Qur’an</strong> in which Abraham explicitly names Muhammad as his son or descendant, nor any verse where Muhammad is directly identified as Abraham’s biological offspring.</p><p>What the Qur’an does affirm is <strong>Abraham’s lineage through Ishmael</strong>, and it places Muhammad within the <strong>broader Abrahamic tradition</strong>, not through an explicit genealogical statement.</p><h3>Key Qur’anic Passages Commonly Cited</h3><ol><li><p><strong>Abraham and Ishmael</strong></p><ul><li><p><em>“And We gave him Isaac and Jacob, and guided them; and Noah We guided before; and among his descendants, David, Solomon… and Ishmael…”</em><br/>(Qur’an 6:84–86)</p></li></ul><p>Ishmael is clearly identified as Abraham’s son. <strong>Muhammad is not mentioned here or elsewhere as Abraham’s son.</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Prayer of Abraham and Ishmael</strong></p><ul><li><p><em>“Our Lord, raise among them a messenger from among themselves who will recite to them Your verses…”</em><br/>(Qur’an 2:129)</p></li></ul><p>This verse is <strong>interpreted by later Islamic tradition</strong> as a reference to Muhammad, but:</p><ul><li><p>Muhammad is <strong>not named</strong></p></li><li><p>No biological lineage is stated</p></li><li><p>The connection is <strong>interpretive, not explicit</strong></p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Religion of Abraham</strong></p><ul><li><p><em>“Then We revealed to you [Muhammad]: Follow the religion of Abraham, inclining toward truth.”</em><br/>(Qur’an 16:123)</p></li></ul><p>This establishes <strong>theological continuity</strong>, not <strong>biological descent</strong>.</p></li><li><p><strong>Muhammad Identified by His Immediate Lineage</strong></p><ul><li><p><em>“Muhammad is not the father of any of your men, but the Messenger of Allah…”</em><br/>(Qur’an 33:40)</p></li></ul><p>Notably, the Qur’an <strong>never traces Muhammad’s genealogy back to Abraham</strong>, despite doing so for other prophets.</p></li></ol><h3>Scholarly Conclusion</h3><ul><li><p>The Qur’an <strong>does not explicitly state</strong> that Muhammad is a son or direct descendant of Abraham.</p></li><li><p>Abraham <strong>never declares Muhammad as his descendant</strong> in the Qur’anic text.</p></li><li><p>The claim that Muhammad descends from Abraham <strong>comes from later Islamic historiography and tradition</strong>, not from a clear Qur’anic assertion.</p></li><li><p>The Qur’an connects Muhammad to Abraham <strong>theologically</strong>, not genealogically.</p></li></ul><p><strong>In summary:</strong><br/>Any assertion that the Qur’an plainly teaches that Muhammad is a direct descendant of Abraham <strong>goes beyond the explicit wording of the Qur’an</strong> and relies on post-Qur’anic tradition rather than direct textual evidence.</p>

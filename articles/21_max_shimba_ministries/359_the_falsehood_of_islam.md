<h3>Reason No.2: THE ONE TRUE GOD</h3>
<p>Muslim preachers often compare Qur’anic and Biblical verses to claim that Allah is the same as Jehovah. Let us examine both the Qur’an and the Bible to see if this claim holds.</p>
<p><strong>Exodus 20:1–3:</strong><br/>
<em>And God spoke all these words, saying, “I am the LORD your God... You shall have no other gods before Me.”</em></p>
<p>The warning means there are other “gods” worshipped by people. The Hebrew “Elohim” and Greek “Theos” appear thousands of times in the Bible, but there are also mentions of false gods:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Dagon</strong> (Judges 16:23)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Baal</strong> (1 Kings 18:21)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Artemis</strong> (Acts 19:24–28)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Similarly, the Qur’an refers to other gods worshipped in Mecca:<br/>
<strong>Qur’an 53:19–23 (Surat An-Najm):</strong><br/>
<em>Have you considered al-Lat and al-‘Uzza and Manat, the third, the other one? ...These are nothing but names you have named, you and your fathers...</em></p>
<p>Thus, different gods are worshipped by different groups. The prophets served Jehovah, but Muhammad worshipped Allah. Is Allah the same as Jehovah?</p>
<p><strong>Deuteronomy 6:4:</strong><br/>
<em>Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one.</em></p>
<p><strong>Isaiah 45:18, 21:</strong><br/>
<em>...I am the LORD, and there is no other.</em></p>
<p><strong>Psalm 86:10:</strong><br/>
<em>For you are great and do marvelous deeds; you alone are God.</em></p>
<p><strong>John 17:3:</strong><br/>
<em>Now this is eternal life: that they know you, the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom you have sent.</em></p>
<p><strong>1 Corinthians 8:4:</strong><br/>
<em>...There is no God but one.</em></p>

<h3>Reason No.4: ALLAH SWEARS BY HIS CREATION, JEHOVAH DOES NOT</h3>
<p><strong>Qur’an 92:1–3 (Surat Al-Layl):</strong><br/>
<em>By the night as it envelops and by the day as it appears...</em></p>
<p><strong>Qur’an 91:1–7 (Surat Ash-Shams):</strong><br/>
<em>By the sun and its brightness... By the soul and Him who proportioned it...</em></p>
<p>Yet, Jehovah does not swear by creation:</p>
<p><strong>Isaiah 44:24:</strong><br/>
<em>I am the LORD, who has made all things, who alone stretched out the heavens, who spread out the earth by myself.</em></p>
<p><strong>Isaiah 45:22–23:</strong><br/>
<em>...For I am God, and there is no other. By myself I have sworn...</em></p>
<p><strong>Hebrews 6:13:</strong><br/>
<em>For when God made a promise to Abraham, because He could swear by no one greater, He swore by Himself...</em></p>
<p>Jehovah swears by Himself, never by creation.</p>

<p><strong>John 3:16–17:</strong><br/>
<em>For God so loved the world that He gave His one and only Son...</em></p>

<h3>Reason No.6: GOD’S NAME</h3>
<p><strong>Qur’an 17:110 (Surat Al-Isra):</strong><br/>
<em>Say, “Call upon Allah or call upon the Most Merciful. Whichever [name] you call—to Him belong the best names...”</em></p>
<p>The name Allah appears 2,866 times in the Qur’an, but the Bible reveals God’s name as Jehovah:</p>
<p><strong>Exodus 6:2–3:</strong><br/>
<em>God spoke to Moses and said, “I am the LORD [YHWH]... I appeared to Abraham... as God Almighty, but by my name Jehovah I was not known to them.”</em></p>
<p><strong>Psalm 83:18:</strong><br/>
<em>That men may know that You, whose name alone is the LORD [YHWH], are the Most High over all the earth.</em></p>
<p>NB: “God” is a title, not a personal name.</p>

<h3>Reason No.7: THE MESSAGE IS DIFFERENT</h3>
<p>While Islamic apologists claim the prophets taught the same message as Muhammad, the evidence is otherwise:</p>
<p><strong>Qur’an 41:6 (Surat Fussilat):</strong><br/>
<em>Say, “I am only a man like you, to whom it has been revealed that your god is one God...”</em></p>
<p><strong>Qur’an 29:46–47 (Surat Al-Ankabut):</strong><br/>
<em>...Our God and your God is one; and to Him we submit.</em></p>
<p>However, the Bible teaches distinct revelation through Jesus Christ and the apostolic church.</p>

<h3>Reason No.8: ANGELS—GABRIEL OR MICHAEL?</h3>
<p>Islam claims Gabriel (Jibril) is the chief angel:</p>
<p><strong>Qur’an 81:19–21 (Surat At-Takwir):</strong><br/>
<em>Indeed, the Qur’an is the word of a noble messenger [Jibril], having power and with the Owner of the Throne, secure [in position]...</em></p>
<p>The Bible presents Michael as the chief angel:</p>
<p><strong>Jude 1:9:</strong><br/>
<em>But even the archangel Michael... said, “The Lord rebuke you!”</em></p>
<p>Therefore, the chief angels differ.</p>

<h3>Reason No.11: PARADISE—ISLAMIC OR CHRISTIAN?</h3>
<p><strong>Luke 20:34–36:</strong><br/>
<em>The people of this age marry and are given in marriage. But those who are considered worthy... will neither marry nor be given in marriage...</em></p>
<p>Islamic Paradise (Jannah) is described as a place of physical pleasures:</p>
<p><strong>Qur’an 39:20:</strong><br/>
<em>But those who fear their Lord will have chambers, above them chambers built high...</em></p>
<p><strong>Qur’an 47:15:</strong><br/>
<em>The description of Paradise... rivers of water... milk... wine... honey... and therein are for them purified spouses...</em></p>
<p><strong>Sunan Ibn Majah, vol. 5, p. 546, Hadith 4337:</strong><br/>
<em>Each man will marry seventy-two wives in Paradise...</em></p>
<p>Christian heaven, in contrast, is spiritual (Romans 14:17; Revelation 7:16–17).</p>

<p> </p><h3 class="" data-end="60" data-start="0"><strong data-end="110" data-start="62">The Shame of the Year for Muhammad and Allah</strong></h3>
<p class="" data-end="172" data-start="112">Does Allah Claim That the World Has Two Easts and Two Wests?</p>
<p class="" data-end="269" data-start="174">Is it true that the world we live in has two Easts and two Wests, or is it a lack of knowledge?</p>
<p class="" data-end="424" data-start="271">Let's start by reading the verse from the Quran that makes such a claim:<br/>
<strong data-end="372" data-start="344">Surah Ar-Rahman, Ayah 17</strong>: "Lord of the two Easts and Lord of the two Wests."</p>
<p class="" data-end="488" data-start="426">Now, let's look at what Earth's science says about directions.</p>
<p class="" data-end="761" data-start="490"><strong data-end="511" data-start="490">What is the East?</strong><br/>
East is one of the four cardinal directions. Its direction is where the sun rises in the morning.<br/>
The name "East" comes from the Arabic word <strong data-end="661" data-start="653">مشرق</strong> (mashriq), which means "the place where the sun rises."<br/>
East is usually on the right side of a map.</p>
<p class="" data-end="1081" data-start="763"><strong data-end="784" data-start="763">What is the West?</strong><br/>
West is one of the four cardinal directions. Its direction is where the sun sets.<br/>
West is also the name of the evening prayer time for Muslims.<br/>
The name "West" comes from the Arabic word <strong data-end="983" data-start="972">مَغْرِب</strong> (maghrib), which means "the place where the sun sets."<br/>
West is usually on the left side of a map.</p>
<p class="" data-end="1315" data-start="1083"><strong data-end="1105" data-start="1083">What is a Compass?</strong><br/>
A compass is a device used to determine direction. It is essential for navigation at sea or on land without visible landmarks like in a desert.<br/>
A compass has four main directions: North, East, South, and West.</p>
<p class="" data-end="1583" data-start="1317">Intermediate directions are mentioned as northeast/northwest and southeast/southwest (in English, Northeast NE/Northwest NW and Southeast SE/Southwest SW).<br/>
It is possible to mention more precise directions, for example, south-southwest (SSW) or west-southwest (WSW).</p>
<p class="" data-end="1712" data-start="1585"><strong data-end="1614" data-start="1585">Surah Al-Ma'arij, Ayah 40</strong>: "But no! I swear by the Lord of all points in the East and the West that We are certainly able."</p>
<p class="" data-end="1864" data-start="1714">Here, Muhammad, who is the speaker of the Quran, contradicts his initial claim that there are two Easts and two Wests in <strong data-end="1863" data-start="1835">Surah Ar-Rahman, Ayah 17</strong>.</p>

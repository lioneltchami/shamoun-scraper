# There Was No Islam Before the Birth of Muhammad

**Category:** 21 Max Shimba Ministries

<p></p><h1>There Was No Islam Before the Birth of Muhammad: A Scholarly Examination</h1><p><strong>By Dr. Maxwell Shimba, Shimba Theological Institute</strong></p><hr/><h2>Abstract</h2><p>A brief summary of the thesis: Islam did not exist prior to Muhammad. No textual, manuscript, archaeological, or linguistic evidence demonstrates the presence of a religion called Islam before the 7th century CE. The Qur’an itself paradoxically identifies Muhammad as the <em>first Muslim</em>, even while retrospectively labeling Abraham and other prophets as Muslims. This paper critically evaluates the historical, theological, and linguistic claims surrounding Islam’s origins and demonstrates that Islam is a distinct 7th-century Arabian development.</p><hr/><h2>Introduction</h2><ul><li><p>State the Islamic theological claim: all prophets were Muslims.</p></li><li><p>Contrast with historical scholarship: no pre-Muhammad Islam is attested.</p></li><li><p>Outline methodology: Qur’anic exegesis, historical-critical analysis, manuscript evidence, archaeology, linguistics.</p></li><li><p>Research question: Did Islam exist before Muhammad, or is it a product of his prophetic career?</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Qur’anic Self-Contradiction</h2><ul><li><p>Surah al-An‘am 6:14 – Muhammad as the first Muslim.</p></li><li><p>Surah az-Zumar 39:1–2 – Islam revealed to Muhammad.</p></li><li><p>Qur’an’s retroactive labeling of Abraham (3:67) and Jesus’ disciples (3:52) as Muslims.</p></li><li><p>Tafsir tradition (Al-Qurtubi, Ibn Kathir) admits Muhammad is “the first” in his era, raising theological contradictions.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Absence of Manuscript Evidence</h2><ul><li><p>Survey of ancient texts: Dead Sea Scrolls, Septuagint, Targums, Mishnah, early Christian Fathers.</p></li><li><p>None mention Islam or “Muslims.”</p></li><li><p>Christian, Jewish, and pagan writers discussing Arabs never describe Islam before 610 CE.</p></li><li><p>Quranic codification only began after Muhammad’s death (Uthman’s recension c. 650 CE).</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Archaeological Silence</h2><ul><li><p>Pre-Islamic Arabia dominated by polytheism (Hubal, al-Lat, al-Uzza, Manat).</p></li><li><p>Kaaba housed 360 idols (Ibn Ishaq, <em>Sirat Rasul Allah</em>).</p></li><li><p>Petra inscriptions and Nabataean traditions reflect Arab paganism, not Islam.</p></li><li><p>No inscriptions or coins referencing Islam before Muhammad.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Linguistic Analysis of <em>Islam</em> and <em>Muslim</em></h2><ul><li><p>Root <em>S-L-M</em> existed in Semitic languages (Hebrew <em>shalom</em>, Aramaic <em>shlama</em>).</p></li><li><p>But as a religious identifier, <em>Islam</em> is novel to the Qur’an.</p></li><li><p>Comparative linguistics: Christianity (<em>Christianoi</em>) and Judaism (<em>Ioudaios</em>) appear in external sources before their consolidation—Islam does not.</p></li><li><p>First external references to Islam appear only in Greek and Syriac sources after Muhammad’s death.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Comparative Abrahamic Traditions</h2><ul><li><p>Judaism: historically verifiable as a covenantal religion of Israel.</p></li><li><p>Christianity: rooted in the historical Jesus, early manuscripts from the 1st–2nd century.</p></li><li><p>Islam: lacks pre-7th century evidence, relies on retroactive theological projection.</p></li><li><p>The attempt to “Islamize” Abraham, Moses, and Jesus is doctrinal—not historical.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Review of Critical Scholarship</h2><ul><li><p>Patricia Crone &amp; Michael Cook (<em>Hagarism</em>): Islam emerged from Arab monotheist reform, not from Abraham.</p></li><li><p>Fred Donner (<em>Muhammad and the Believers</em>): early movement was broader and only later crystallized as “Islam.”</p></li><li><p>Robert Hoyland (<em>Seeing Islam as Others Saw It</em>): non-Muslim sources of the 7th century never mention Islam as a religion until decades after Muhammad.</p></li><li><p>Conclusion from scholarship: Islam originated in Muhammad’s lifetime, not before.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Theological and Historical Implications</h2><ul><li><p>Islam’s claim of continuity is theological fiction, not historical fact.</p></li><li><p>If Muhammad is the first Muslim, then pre-Islamic prophets cannot be Muslims.</p></li><li><p>Christianity and Judaism maintain historically verifiable roots predating their founders.</p></li><li><p>Islam stands alone as a 7th-century innovation, contradicting its own claim of eternal continuity.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>Conclusion</h2><ul><li><p>Restate thesis: Islam did not exist before Muhammad.</p></li><li><p>Evidence: Qur’anic contradiction, absence of manuscripts, archaeological silence, linguistic novelty, critical scholarship.</p></li><li><p>Final statement: Islam must be recognized as a uniquely 7th-century Arabian religion, born from Muhammad’s teachings, rather than a timeless Abrahamic faith.</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>References / Bibliography</h2><ul><li><p>Al-Qurtubi. <em>Tafsir al-Qurtubi</em>.

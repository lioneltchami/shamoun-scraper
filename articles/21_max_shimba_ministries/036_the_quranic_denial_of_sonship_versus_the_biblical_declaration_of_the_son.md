# The Quranic Denial of Sonship Versus the Biblical Declaration of the Son

<div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4077015315528416743" itemprop="description articleBody">
<p></p><h1>The Quranic Denial of Sonship</h1><h2>Versus the Biblical Declaration of the Son</h2><p>The divide between Christianity and Islam is not minor.<br/>It centers on <strong>one question</strong>:</p><blockquote><p><strong>Does God have a Son in His own divine identity?</strong></p></blockquote><p>The Bible answers <strong>yes</strong>.<br/>The Quran answers <strong>no—absolutely not</strong>.</p><hr/><h2>1. What the Quran DENIES about Sonship</h2><p>The Quran repeatedly rejects the idea that God has a Son—but <strong>it rejects a specific misunderstanding of sonship</strong>.</p><h3>A. The Quran denies <strong>biological sonship</strong></h3><p><strong>Surah Al-Ikhlāṣ 112:3</strong></p><blockquote><p>“He neither begets nor is begotten.”</p></blockquote><p><strong>Surah Al-An‘ām 6:101</strong></p><blockquote><p>“How could He have a son when He has no consort?”</p></blockquote><p><strong>Surah Maryam 19:88–92</strong></p><blockquote><p>“They say, ‘The Most Merciful has taken a son.’<br/>You have done an atrocious thing…”</p></blockquote><p><strong>Key assumption in the Quran</strong>:<br/>👉 <em>To have a son means sexual reproduction.</em></p><p>That is the concept the Quran rejects.</p><hr/><h3>B. The Quran treats “Son of God” as pagan mythology</h3><p><strong>Surah Al-Mā’idah 5:72</strong></p><blockquote><p>“They have certainly disbelieved who say, ‘Allah is the Messiah, son of Mary.’”</p></blockquote><p><strong>Surah At-Tawbah 9:30</strong></p><blockquote><p>“The Christians say, ‘The Messiah is the son of Allah’… may Allah destroy them.”</p></blockquote><p>The Quran places Christian belief in the same category as:</p><ul><li><p>Pagan gods having offspring</p></li><li><p>Mythological procreation</p></li><li><p>Created beings elevated improperly</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>2. What the Bible MEANS by Sonship (which the Quran never addresses)</h2><p>Here is the critical failure:<br/><strong>The Quran never engages the biblical definition of Sonship.</strong></p><h3>Biblical Sonship is:</h3><ul><li><p><strong>Eternal</strong>, not biological</p></li><li><p><strong>Ontological</strong>, not physical</p></li><li><p><strong>Relational</strong>, not sexual</p></li></ul><hr/><h3>A. Sonship means sharing God’s nature</h3><p><strong>John 5:18</strong></p><blockquote><p>“He was calling God His own Father, <strong>making Himself equal with God</strong>.”</p></blockquote><p>The Jews understood perfectly:</p><ul><li><p>“Son of God” = <strong>equality of nature</strong></p></li><li><p>Not adoption</p></li><li><p>Not creation</p></li></ul><hr/><h3>B. Sonship is eternal, not created</h3><p><strong>John 1:18</strong></p><blockquote><p>“The only-begotten God, who is at the Father’s side…”</p></blockquote><p>“Begotten” here means:</p><ul><li><p>From the Father’s own being</p></li><li><p>Not made</p></li><li><p>Not in time</p></li></ul><hr/><h3>C. Sonship means sharing God’s Name and Glory</h3><p><strong>John 17:5</strong></p><blockquote><p>“Father, glorify Me with the glory I had with You <strong>before the world existed</strong>.”</p></blockquote><p>No prophet speaks like this.<br/>No angel claims pre-creation glory with God.</p><hr/><h2>3. Why the Quran MUST deny Sonship</h2><p>If the Quran accepted biblical Sonship, three pillars of Islam would collapse:</p><h3>1. <strong>Tawḥīd as absolute singularity</strong></h3><p>Biblical monotheism is <strong>unity of essence</strong>, not numerical solitude.</p><h3>2. <strong>Muhammad as final messenger</strong></h3><p>Hebrews 1:2 says God has spoken <strong>finally in His Son</strong>.</p><h3>3. <strong>Jesus as only a prophet</strong></h3><p>But prophets do not:</p><ul><li><p>Share God’s Name (Philippians 2)</p></li><li><p>Receive worship (Matthew 14:33)</p></li><li><p>Create the universe (Hebrews 1:10)</p></li></ul><hr/><h2>4. The irony: the Quran refutes a belief Christians never held</h2><p>Christians have <strong>never taught</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>God had a wife</p></li><li><p>God produced a son sexually</p></li><li><p>God generated offspring biologically</p></li></ul><p>Yet the Quran argues as if this were the Christian claim.</p><p><strong>This is a category error.</strong></p><p>The Quran rejects:</p><blockquote><p><strong>Pagan sonship</strong></p></blockquote><p>The Bible teaches:</p><blockquote><p><strong>Eternal divine Sonship</strong></p></blockquote><p>They are not the same concept.</p><hr/><h2>5. The unavoidable conclusion</h2><ul><li><p>The Quran denies sonship because it <strong>misdefines it</strong></p></li><li><p>The Bible affirms sonship because it <strong>defines it theologically</strong></p></li></ul><p>That is why:</p><ul><li><p>Islam must say: <em>“Allah has no son.”</em></p></li><li><p>Jesus can say:</p><blockquote><p><strong>“Baptize in the NAME of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.”</strong></p></blockquote></li></ul><p>Because in biblical theology:</p><blockquote><p><strong>To be Son is to share the one divine essence.</strong></p></blockquote><hr/><h2>Final challenge</h2><p>Islam is correct to say:</p><blockquote><p>“No creature can be God’s son.”</p></blockquote><p>Christianity agrees.</p><p>That is precisely why Jesus is not a creature.</p><p>The real question is not:</p><blockquote><p><em>“Does God have a Son?”</em></p></blockquote><p>But:</p><blockquote><p><strong>“Is Jesus who He claimed to be?”</strong></p></blockquote><p>Because if He is,<br/>then denying the Son is not preserving monotheism—</p><p>It is <strong>rejecting God’s own self-revelation</strong>.</p><hr/><p><br/></p>

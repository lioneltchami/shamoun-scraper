<h3><strong>A Theological Examination of the Biblical God (YHWH) and the Quranic Allah</strong></h3>
<p><em>By Dr. Maxwell Shimba, Shimba Theological Institute</em></p>
<p>The identity of God remains one of the most profound subjects of inquiry in comparative theology. Within Judeo-Christian tradition, God reveals Himself as <strong>YHWH (Yahweh)</strong>—the covenant-keeping, self-existent One who declares, “I AM WHO I AM” (Exodus 3:14). In contrast, the <strong>Quranic depiction of Allah</strong> presents a being whose attributes, actions, and relationship with humanity differ substantially from the Biblical revelation of God.</p>
<p>From a <strong>Biblical standpoint</strong>, Yahweh is not only the Creator but also a <strong>relational and redemptive</strong> God, revealed through His Son, <strong>Jesus Christ</strong> (John 1:1-14). Christianity holds that God’s ultimate revelation is incarnated in Christ, who embodies divine love and truth. Conversely, <strong>Islamic theology explicitly denies</strong> the divinity and sonship of Jesus (Quran 4:171), emphasizing absolute monotheism (tawḥīd) in a way that excludes any Trinitarian understanding.</p>
<p>Scholars such as <strong>Norman Geisler</strong> and <strong>Abdul Saleeb</strong> (<em>Answering Islam: The Crescent in Light of the Cross</em>, Baker, 2002) have argued that, although the term <em>God</em> may appear linguistically similar, the <strong>ontological essence and moral attributes</strong> of Allah and Yahweh diverge significantly. The <strong>Biblical God</strong> reveals Himself as <strong>love</strong> (1 John 4:8), while the <strong>Quranic Allah</strong> is primarily characterized by <strong>power and transcendence</strong> rather than personal communion.</p>
<p>Therefore, the assertion that Allah and Yahweh are the same being is theologically untenable within classical Christian doctrine. Any attempt to conflate the two diminishes the <strong>uniqueness of the Trinitarian revelation</strong> and the redemptive role of Jesus Christ. For Christians, <strong>Yahweh alone</strong> is the one true God, self-revealed in the Scriptures and in His Son—the incarnate Word.</p>

<p> </p><header class="entry-header" style='background-color: white; box-sizing: inherit; color: #555555; font-family: "Source Sans Pro", sans-serif; font-size: 14px;'><h1 class="entry-title" style='box-sizing: inherit; clear: both; color: #444444; font-family: "Open Sans", sans-serif; font-size: 2.25rem; line-height: 1.4; margin: 0px 0px 1.1rem; overflow-wrap: break-word;'>MUHAMMAD’S TALKING ANIMAL FARM</h1></header><div class="entry-content" style='background-color: white; box-sizing: inherit; color: #555555; counter-reset: footnotes 0; font-family: "Source Sans Pro", sans-serif; font-size: 1.125rem; overflow-wrap: break-word;'><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px 0px 1.1em;">According to the most reliable collection of sunni hadiths, Muhammad believed that animals like cows and wolves could actually speak like humans do!  </p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 1em 0px 1.1em;">60 Prophets</p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 1em 0px 1.1em;">(54) Chapter:</p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 1em 0px 1.1em;">Narrated Abu Huraira:</p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 1em 0px 1.1em;">Once Allah’s Messenger offered the morning prayer and then faced the people and said, “While a man was driving a cow, he suddenly rode over it and beat it. <strong style="box-sizing: inherit; font-weight: bold;">The cow said, ‘We have not been created for this, but we have been created for sloughing</strong>.’ On that the people said astonishingly, “Glorified be Allah! <strong style="box-sizing: inherit; font-weight: bold;">A cow speaks!</strong>” The Prophet said, “<strong style="box-sizing: inherit; font-weight: bold;">I believe this, and Abu Bakr and `Umar too, believe it, although neither of them was present there</strong>.” While a person was amongst his sheep, a wolf attacked and took one of the sheep. The man chased the wolf till he saved it from the wolf, <strong style="box-sizing: inherit; font-weight: bold;">where upon the wolf said, ‘You have saved it from me; but who will guard it on the day of the wild beasts when there will be no shepherd to guard them except me (because of riots and afflictions)?</strong>” The people said surprisingly, “Glorified be Allah! <strong style="box-sizing: inherit; font-weight: bold;">A wolf speaks!</strong>” The Prophet said, “<strong style="box-sizing: inherit; font-weight: bold;">But I believe this, and Abu Bakr and `Umar too, believe this</strong>, although neither of them was present there.” (See the Foot-note of page No. 10 Vol.5)</p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 1em 0px 1.1em;"><strong style="box-sizing: inherit; font-weight: bold;">Reference</strong>: Sahih al-Bukhari 3471</p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 1em 0px 1.1em;">In-book reference: Book 60, Hadith 138</p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 1em 0px 1.1em;">USC-MSA web (English) reference: Vol. 4, Book 55, Hadith 677 (sunnah.com <a href="https://sunnah.com/bukhari:3471" style="background-color: transparent; border-bottom: 1px dotted rgb(170, 170, 170); box-sizing: inherit; color: #444444; text-decoration-line: none;">https://sunnah.com/bukhari:3471</a>)</p></div>

<p>Dear Reader,<br/>
Today, I would like us to study about <em>Muhammad and the Use of Witchcraft in Islam</em>.</p>
<h3>MUHAMMAD USED WITCHCRAFT</h3>
<p>Muhammad used witchcraft (magical powers) against the evil eye and venomous creatures like lizards. <em>(Ibn-i-Majah, Vol. 5, No. 3525, p. 48).</em><br/>
Now, where do we read in the <em>Torah</em>, <em>Psalms</em>, or <em>Gospel</em> that the Prophets and Apostles of the Bible used witchcraft?<br/>
Does Allah not have the power to heal the evil eye or poison without using witchcraft?</p>
<h3>MUHAMMAD GAVE HIS WIFE AISHA A TALISMAN</h3>
<p>Muhammad gave his wife ‘Aisha a magical charm to heal her from the evil eye. <em>(Sahih Muslim, Vol. 3, Book 24, Nos. 5445, 5447–5450, p. 1196).</em><br/>
What is the difference between witchcraft and sorcery? As we see above, Muhammad gave witchcraft to his beloved wife.<br/>
Where do we ever read that the Prophets of the Bible used witchcraft?</p>
<p>Muhammad believed in the evil eye and had charms against it. <em>(Al-Tabari, Vol. 39, p. 134).</em><br/>
The last two chapters of the Qur’an (Surahs 113 and 114) were written concerning the evil eye according to <em>(Ibn-i-Majah, Vol. 5, No. 3517, p. 41).</em></p>
<p>However, Surahs 113 and 114 are <em>missing</em> from Ibn Mas’ud’s version of the Qur’an <em>(The Fihrist, p. 57).</em> This omission was not accidental. It is recorded that Ibn Mas’ud said:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The two charms—Surahs [113, 114]—are not part of the Book of God.”</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Ibn Mas’ud was Muhammad’s personal secretary. Muhammad told people to learn the Qur’an from Ibn Mas’ud and three others. <em>(Bukhari, Vol. 6, Book 60, Section 8, No. 521, pp. 486–487).</em></p>
<h3>MUHAMMAD SAID THAT TALISMANS CURE THE EVIL EYE</h3>
<p>He claimed that witchcraft (charms) cures the evil eye. <em>(Ibn-i-Majah, Vol. 5, Nos. 3510, 3511, p. 41; Vol. 5, No. 3512, p. 42).</em></p>
<h3>AL-BADIRI IS A FORM OF MAGIC</h3>
<p>Al-Badiri was permitted as protection against the evil eye according to <em>(Ibn-i-Majah, Vol. 5, Nos. 3513–3518, pp. 42–44).</em></p>
<p>The evil eye is treated as a real condition. <em>(Bukhari, Vol. 7, Book 71, Sections 36 &amp; 86, Nos. 636, 827, pp. 427, 538).</em><br/>
Also read <em>(Bukhari, Vol. 4, Book 55, Section 9, No. 590, p. 386).</em><br/>
The evil eye is indeed real. <em>(Ibn-i-Majah, Vol. 5, Nos. 3506, 3507, p. 39).</em></p>
<blockquote>
<p>“The influence of the evil eye is a fact.”<br/>
—<em>(Sahih Muslim, Vol. 3, Book 24, No. 5426, p. 1192; also Nos. 5424–5427, p. 1192).</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>It is said that Amir bin Rabia cast the evil eye upon a man. <em>(Ibn-i-Majah, Vol. 5, No. 3508, p. 40).</em></p>
<p>However, Muhammad’s hair was believed to cure the evil eye. <em>(Bukhari, Vol. 7, Book 72, Section 65, No. 784, p. 518).</em><br/>
When Muhammad’s hair was shaved, his followers rushed to grab every strand to keep it as a sacred relic. Muhammad kindly distributed his hair among them. <em>(Sahih Muslim, Vol. 2, Book 7, Nos. 2991–2994, pp. 656–657).</em></p>
<h3>MAGIC THROUGH CUPPING ON SPECIFIC DATES</h3>
<p>Muhammad also said that if people perform <em>cupping</em> (bloodletting) on the 17th, 19th, or 21st day of the lunar month, it will cure every disease. <em>(Abu Dawud, Vol. 3, No. 3852, p. 1084).</em></p>
<p>If Muhammad was truly a Prophet of God, why did he practice witchcraft?<br/>
If Allah is truly God, why would He allow the use of witchcraft?<br/>
If Islam comes from God, why does it allow magical charms?</p>

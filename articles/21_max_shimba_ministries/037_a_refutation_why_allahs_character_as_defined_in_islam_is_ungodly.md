<div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3836926775674376654" itemprop="description articleBody">
<p></p><h3>A Refutation: Why Allah’s Character (as Defined in Islam) Is Ungodly</h3><p>The statement that <em>“Allah does not love sinners, only those who repent”</em> reveals a <strong>serious moral and theological deficiency</strong> in the Islamic concept of God. It does not defend Allah’s holiness; it exposes his <strong>lack of redemptive love</strong>.</p><h4>1. Love Reduced to Approval Is Not Divine Love</h4><p>Islam claims that Allah’s love means <em>approval, pleasure, or acceptance</em>, not relational or sacrificial love. This is precisely the problem.</p><p>A god who only “loves” what already conforms to his demands is not loving—he is <strong>transactional</strong>. This is not holiness; it is <strong>conditional favoritism</strong>.</p><p>In contrast, the Bible reveals a God whose love is <strong>initiating</strong>, <strong>restorative</strong>, and <strong>self-giving</strong>:</p><blockquote><p>“But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.” (Romans 5:8)</p></blockquote><p>Biblically, God does not love sin—but He <strong>does love sinners</strong>, precisely <em>because they are lost</em>. Love that waits for repentance before it exists is not love; it is <strong>reward</strong>.</p><hr/><h4>2. Allah’s Love Is Reactive, Not Redemptive</h4><p>The Qur’an repeatedly states that Allah only loves people <em>after</em> they repent (Qur’an 2:222), meaning:</p><ul><li><p>No love <strong>before</strong> repentance</p></li><li><p>No love <strong>during</strong> brokenness</p></li><li><p>No love <strong>toward the lost</strong></p></li></ul><p>This makes repentance a <strong>precondition for love</strong>, not a response to love.</p><p>Biblically, repentance flows <strong>from</strong> God’s love, not <em>toward</em> it:</p><blockquote><p>“Do you despise the riches of His goodness… not knowing that the goodness of God leads you to repentance?” (Romans 2:4)</p></blockquote><p>Allah demands repentance <strong>to earn love</strong>.<br/>The God of Scripture gives love <strong>to produce repentance</strong>.</p><hr/><h4>3. Mercy Without Love Is Empty</h4><p>Islam often substitutes <em>mercy</em> for love, but mercy without love is merely <strong>temporary suspension of punishment</strong>, not transformation.</p><p>Allah forgives without entering history.<br/>Allah pardons without sacrifice.<br/>Allah shows mercy without self-giving.</p><p>This is not moral greatness—it is <strong>detached authority</strong>.</p><p>The biblical God absorbs the cost of forgiveness Himself:</p><blockquote><p>“The Son of Man came to seek and to save that which was lost.” (Luke 19:10)</p></blockquote><blockquote><p>“Greater love has no one than this, than to lay down one’s life for his friends.” (John 15:13)</p></blockquote><hr/><h4>4. A God Who Does Not Love the Sinner Is Not Good</h4><p>If Allah does not love sinners <strong>as sinners</strong>, then:</p><ul><li><p>He does not love the weak</p></li><li><p>He does not love the broken</p></li><li><p>He does not love humanity as it truly is</p></li></ul><p>Such a being may be powerful—but <strong>not morally perfect</strong>.</p><p>Jesus explicitly contradicts this view:</p><blockquote><p>“Love your enemies… that you may be sons of your Father in heaven.” (Matthew 5:44–45)</p></blockquote><p>If humans are commanded to love enemies, yet Allah does not, then <strong>Allah falls below the moral standard he supposedly authored</strong>.</p><hr/><h4>5. The Islamic Position Confuses Holiness With Absence of Love</h4><p>Islam argues that loving sinners would mean approving sin. This is a <strong>false dilemma</strong>.</p><p>The Bible clearly separates:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Loving the person</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Hating the sin</strong></p></li></ul><p>Only an ungodly concept of deity collapses these into one.</p><p>Jesus ate with sinners <em>without approving sin</em>.<br/>Allah, by contrast, withholds love until moral correction occurs.</p><p>That is not holiness.<br/>That is <strong>distance</strong>.</p><hr/><h3>Conclusion: The Issue Is Not Education—It Is Revelation</h3><p>This debate is not about ignorance; it is about <strong>which God has revealed Himself</strong>.</p><ul><li><p>Allah loves the morally acceptable</p></li><li><p>The God of the Bible loves the morally broken</p></li></ul><p>One demands ascent.<br/>The other descends.</p><p>One waits for repentance.<br/>The other <strong>creates it through love</strong>.</p><p>A god who cannot love sinners until they change is <strong>not the God who saves sinners</strong>.</p><blockquote><p>“For God so loved the world…” (John 3:16)<br/>—not the repented world,<br/>—not the purified world,<br/>—but the <strong>fallen world</strong>.</p></blockquote><p>That is the difference between <strong>ungodly authority</strong> and <strong>holy love</strong>.</p>

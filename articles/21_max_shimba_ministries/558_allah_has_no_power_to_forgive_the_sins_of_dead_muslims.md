<p><strong>ALLAH HAS NO POWER TO FORGIVE THE SINS OF DEAD MUSLIMS</strong></p>
<p>Does Allah have the power to forgive people’s sins?</p>
<p>My brothers and sisters,</p>
<p>Today I would like us to examine the Allah of the Islamic religion, whom they claim is their God. Does this Allah have the authority to forgive all sins?</p>
<p>The Quran repeatedly states that those who die in their sins can never be forgiven by Allah, even though Muslims still have the tradition of praying for the dead so that Allah may receive the soul of the deceased.</p>
<p>Surat Al-Mu’minun 101: <em>“Then when the Trumpet is blown, there will be no kinship among them on that Day, nor will they ask one another.”</em><br/>
102: <em>“Those whose scales are heavy, they are the successful.”</em><br/>
103: <em>“But those whose scales are light, they are the ones who have lost their souls; in Hell they will abide forever.”</em><br/>
104: <em>“The Fire will scorch their faces, and they will be disfigured.”</em><br/>
105: <em>“Were not My verses recited to you, but you denied them?”</em><br/>
106: <em>“Our Lord! Our misfortune overwhelmed us, and we were a misguided people.”</em><br/>
107: <em>“Our Lord! Bring us out of this. If we return (to evil), then indeed we will be wrongdoers.”</em><br/>
108: <em>“He (Allah) will say: Stay therein, and do not speak to Me.”</em><br/>
109–115: (Summary) Allah praises a group that remained faithful, but rejects the others, saying they stayed in the world only briefly and will return to Him for judgment.</p>
<p>In this chapter, we see Muslims who perished in their sins. Allah rejects them and refuses to forgive them, even though their relatives prayed for them at death and performed cleansing rituals. Allah plainly states that He will <strong>not</strong> forgive these Muslims. Additional evidence can be found in S. 35:36–37, S. 40:10–12, S. 43:74–77, S. 67:6–11, and S. 4:93.</p>
<hr/>
<h3>**ALLAH DOES NOT FORGIVE A MUSLIM WHO KILLS ANOTHER MUSLIM:</h3>
<p>EXAMPLE — SUNNI KILLING SHIA**</p>
<p>Allah says that if a Muslim kills another Muslim intentionally, Allah will <em>never</em> forgive that sin.</p>
<p>Surat An-Nisa 93:<br/>
<em>“And whoever kills a believer intentionally, his recompense is Hell, to abide therein forever. Allah is angry with him, curses him, and has prepared for him a great punishment.”</em></p>
<p>We have overwhelming evidence of Muslims killing one another— especially through bombings, warfare, and sectarian conflict. See news from the Middle East for more proof.</p>
<p>If Sunnis and Shias kill each other, where will they all end up? Allah already said <strong>they will all be thrown into Hell</strong> and He will never forgive them (Surat An-Nisa 93). Perhaps this is why Allah declared that all Muslims will enter Hell:</p>
<p>Quran 40:60:<br/>
<em>“And your Lord says: Call upon Me; I will respond to you. But those who are too arrogant to worship Me will enter Hell humiliated.”</em></p>
<p>My brothers and sisters, Allah concludes in Surah 40:60 that all who boast in Islam will enter Hell. So will any Muslim escape? It becomes clear that Allah contradicts himself about forgiveness because he already decreed that all Muslims will enter Hell.</p>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>Even Allah Himself Will Enter Hell</strong></h3>
<p>Now read Sahih Bukhari:</p>
<p><strong>Bukhari, Vol. 6, Book 60, Hadith 371</strong> — narrated by Anas:<br/>
<em>“People will be thrown into Hell, and Hell will say: ‘Are there any more?’ until Allah will place His foot upon Hell and it will say, ‘Enough! Enough!’”</em></p>
<p>Other references:<br/>
Bukhari Vol. 9 Book 93 Hadith 481<br/>
Bukhari Vol. 6 Book 60 Hadith 373</p>
<p>This reveals a great secret: <strong>Allah himself enters Hell.</strong><br/>
Is this not a great tragedy for the religion of Muhammad, that even Allah goes to Hell?</p>
<p>The Bible says Satan also enters the lake of fire last— just like Allah entering last when Hell asks for “more.”</p>
<p>Revelation 20:10:<br/>
<em>“And the devil who deceived them was thrown into the lake of fire… and will be tormented day and night forever and ever.”</em></p>
<p>The Bible shows that the Devil enters Hell and finds others already there—exactly like the Hadith says Allah enters Hell and finds people inside.</p>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>MORE VERSES WHERE ALLAH ADMITS HE CANNOT FORGIVE SINS</strong></h3>
<p>Now examine Surat Al-Imran:</p>
<p>3:86 — Allah says He will not guide those who believed then disbelieved, even after clear signs.<br/>
3:87 — They will have Allah’s curse and that of angels and all mankind.<br/>
3:88 — They will remain in punishment forever; no reduction and no delay.</p>
<p>But 3:89 says:<br/>
<em>“Except those who repent afterwards and reform; Allah is Forgiving and Merciful.”</em></p>
<p>Here Allah contradicts himself:<br/>
— In verse 88 he says they <em>will never</em> be forgiven.<br/>
— In verse 89 he suddenly changes and says “if they repent, they will be forgiven.”</p>
<p>Was Allah angry during revelation? Did he forget what he said in verse 88? Or is this the confusion and contradiction that fills the Quran?</p>
<p>This passage clearly states that <strong>disbelievers who die as unbelievers will never be forgiven</strong>, even if they wish to return to Allah. Their repentance is rejected forever.</p>
<p>But Muslims, according to the tafsir, may receive reduced punishment and temporary Hell.</p>

<p>Luke 7:48:<br/>
<em>“Then He said to the woman, ‘Your sins are forgiven.’”</em></p>
<p>Mark 2:10:<br/>
<em>“But that you may know that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins…”</em></p>

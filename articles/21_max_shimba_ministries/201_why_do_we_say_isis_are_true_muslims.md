<p>In the city of Medina, there was a Jewish tribe known as <strong>Banu Qurayza</strong>. They were attacked by Muhammad and his group in a battle that lasted about 25 days. Eventually, they were overpowered and <strong>surrendered</strong>.</p>
<p>Muhammad chose one of his companions, <strong>Sa’d ibn Mu’adh</strong>, to determine what should happen after Banu Qurayza surrendered.<br/>
Sa’d ibn Mu’adh ruled that:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>all the men of Banu Qurayza should be executed,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>their property confiscated,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>their women and children taken as captives.</p>
</li>

<p>Muhammad <strong>accepted</strong> this ruling.</p>
<p>So what happened to those who were sentenced to death?</p>
<p>We read in <strong>Al-Tabari 8:35</strong> and <strong>Ibn Ishaq 464</strong>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>“The Jews were made to come down, and Allah's Messenger imprisoned them. Then the Prophet went out into the marketplace of Medina, and he <strong>had trenches dug</strong> in it. He sent for the Jewish men and had them <strong>beheaded</strong> in those trenches. They were brought out to him in batches. They numbered <strong>800 to 900 boys and men</strong>.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Meaning:</p>
<p>The Jews were brought, and the Messenger of Allah imprisoned them. Then the Prophet went to the marketplace of Medina and ordered trenches to be dug. He commanded that the Jewish men be brought to him, and he ordered their <strong>beheading</strong> in those trenches. They were brought to him in groups. Their total number reached <strong>800 to 900 men and boys</strong>.</p>
<p>This is the Muhammad whom Muslims claim is the beloved of Allah and the <strong>perfect example</strong> to follow.</p>

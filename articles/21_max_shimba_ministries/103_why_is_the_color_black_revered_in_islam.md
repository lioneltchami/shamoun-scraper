<h1><strong>What Does Color Represent Biblically?</strong></h1>
<p>God uses colors in Scripture to communicate spiritual messages. For example, after Noah left the ark, God placed the <strong>rainbow</strong>—a combination of seven colors—as a covenant that He would never again destroy the earth with a flood.<br/>
Why specifically <strong>seven colors</strong>? Why not another sign or an image?<br/>
Because <strong>colors carry deep spiritual meaning in the realm of the Spirit.</strong></p>
<h3><strong>Biblical Meanings of Colors</strong></h3>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>1. Green</strong></h3>
<p>Green represents <strong>life, prosperity, freshness, and fruitfulness</strong>. Where there is life, there is greenness. When rain falls, plants sprout and appear green, symbolizing blessing and favor.</p>
<p><strong>Genesis 1:11</strong> – “Then God said, ‘Let the earth bring forth grass…’”<br/>
Thus, in visions or dreams, green signifies <strong>grace and blessing ahead</strong>.</p>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>2. Blue</strong></h3>
<p>Blue represents <strong>the glory of God</strong>.</p>
<p>“...When I consider your heavens, the work of your fingers…”<br/>
—<strong>Psalm 8:3</strong></p>
<p>Large bodies of water—lakes, seas—appear blue from a distance. When God shows you blue in a vision, He is revealing <strong>His glory and majesty</strong>.</p>
<hr/>
<h3><strong>3. White</strong></h3>
<p>White represents <strong>purity, holiness, and divine light</strong>.</p>
<p>Where there is whiteness, nothing can hide; even dirt is easily exposed. God often reveals Himself in white garments, white hair, and heavenly brightness.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Revelation 1:14 – Christ with white hair</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Daniel 7:9 – The Ancient of Days, with white clothing</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Revelation 20:11 – The White Throne</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Revelation 3:5 – Saints given white garments</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Heaven itself is associated with this pure brilliance.<br/>
White symbolizes <strong>holiness, purity, and the presence of the Holy Spirit</strong>.</p>

<p>Black represents <strong>darkness</strong>, because darkness is visually black.<br/>
Evil deeds are done in darkness.</p>

<p>Thus, black symbolizes <strong>the kingdom of darkness and the authority of Satan</strong>. So, when one encounters black in a spiritual dream—such as the testimony above—one must seek God for guidance in spiritual warfare, because satanic forces may be involved.</p>

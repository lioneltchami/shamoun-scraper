<p><strong>Question for Muslim Scholars and Believers:</strong><br/>
In Islamic theology, the <em>Injeel</em> (Gospel) is described as a divine revelation given by Allah. According to the Qur’an, the <em>Injeel</em> was revealed to <strong>ʿIsa (Jesus)</strong>, who, as even the Qur’an affirms, was <strong>sent to the Children of Israel</strong> (see Qur’an 3:49; 61:6).</p>
<p>Therefore, a critical question arises:<br/>
<strong>To which community was the Injeel originally addressed?</strong><br/>
A. The <strong>Jews</strong>, to whom Jesus was sent<br/>
B. The <strong>Muslims</strong>, who did not yet exist as a religious group<br/>
C. The <strong>Christians</strong>, who only later emerged as followers of Jesus’ teachings</p>
<p>From a historical and theological standpoint, the answer must logically be <strong>A — the Jews</strong>, since Jesus’ ministry, according to both the Bible (Matthew 15:24) and the Qur’an, was directed to the people of Israel.</p>
<p>This question highlights a fundamental point in comparative theology: <strong>the Injeel was a revelation within the Jewish context, not an Islamic one</strong>, and its message later gave rise to Christianity — centuries before Islam’s formation.</p>

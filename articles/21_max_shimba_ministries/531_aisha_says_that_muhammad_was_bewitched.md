<p>Muhammad was bewitched. ‘Aisha narrated: The Prophet continued for some time believing that he was having sexual relations with his wives when he was not. One day he said to me:<br/>
“O ‘Aisha! God has instructed me concerning the matter I asked Him about. Two men came; one sat at my feet and the other near my head. The one near my feet said to the one near my head (pointing at me), ‘What is wrong with this man?’ The other replied, ‘He has been affected by magic.’ The first asked, ‘Who cast the spell on him?’ The other replied, ‘Lubaid bin A’sam.’ The first asked, ‘With what (material)?’ The other replied, ‘With the pollen of a male date palm and a comb, in which hair was placed, and buried under a stone in the well of Dharwan.’”</p>
<p>Then the Prophet went to that well and said:<br/>
“This is the same well that was shown to me in the dream. The tops of its date palms look like the heads of devils, and its water looks like a mixture of henna.”<br/>
‘Aisha added: “The magician Lubaid bin A’sam was from the tribe of Bani Zuraiq, an ally of the Jews.”<br/>
<strong>Bukhari</strong>, Volume 8, Book 73 (Good Manners), Chapter 56, no. 89, p. 57.<br/>
See also <strong>Bukhari</strong>, Volume 8, Book 75 (Book of Invocations), Chapter 59, no. 400, p. 266.</p>
